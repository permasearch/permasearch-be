Ahead of the new season, it was announced that Walcott had extended his contract with Arsenal after prolonged negotiations.[106] Walcott netted twice in pre-season in wins against Everton and Wolfsburg, as Arsenal won the Premier League Asia Trophy and the Emirates Cup.[107][108] He began the 2015–16 season, starting at centre-forward and assisting Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain's winning goal in the 1–0 defeat of Chelsea in the 2015 FA Community Shield on 2 August 2015.[109][110]
