On January 24, 2017, Microsoft showcased Intune for Education at the BETT 2017 education technology conference in London.[117] Intune for Education is a new cloud-based application and device management service for the education sector.[118] Microsoft will launch a preview of Intune for Education "in the coming weeks", with general availability scheduled for spring 2017, priced at $30 per device, or through volume licensing agreements.[119] On June 8, 2017, Microsoft acquired Hexadite, an Israeli security firm, for $100 million.[120][121]
