According to the Boston Public Health Commission, 264 civilians were treated at 27 local hospitals.[5][265] Eleven days later, 29 remained hospitalized, one in critical condition.[266] Many victims had lower leg injuries and shrapnel wounds,[267] which indicated that the devices were low to the ground.[268] At least 16 civilians lost limbs, at the scene or by amputation in a hospital, and three lost more than one limb.[269][270][271][272]
