Coney Island is served by three New York City Subway stations.[162][163] The Coney Island–Stillwell Avenue station, which is the terminal of the D​, F​, N​, and Q trains, is one of the largest elevated rapid transit stations in the world, with eight tracks serving four platforms.[164] The entire station, built in 1917–1920 as a replacement for the former surface-level Culver Depot,[165] was rebuilt in 2001–2004.[166][164] The other subway stations within Coney Island are West Eighth Street–New York Aquarium, which is served by the F​ and Q trains, and Ocean Parkway, which is served by the Q train.[163]
