The research also suggests that social inhibitions can be divided between different kinds of social fears, or different patterns of inhibition can be seen in individuals. The researchers suggest that certain patters, or certain social fears, can be better predictors of social phobia than others.[47] Mainly, the researchers suggest that there can be different patterns of social inhibition in relation to an unfamiliar object or encounter.[47] These specific patterns should be looked at in conjunction with motivation and the psychophysiological reaction to the object or encounter to determine the specific patterns that are the better predictors of social phobia.[47]
