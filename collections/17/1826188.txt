Romanians (dated: Rumanians or Roumanians; in Romanian: români pronounced [roˈmɨnʲ] or — historically, but now a seldom-used regionalism — rumâni; dated exonym: Vlachs) are a nation and ethnic group native to Central-Eastern Europe, who form the majority of the populations of the present-day states of Romania and Republic of Moldova, sharing a common Romanian culture and speaking Romanian as their native language.
