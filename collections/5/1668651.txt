Today the larger section of the AAC is the rotary-wing part. Its aviators fly four types of helicopter, and within each type there are usually several marks/variants which carry out different roles. Pilots designated for rotary-wing service train at the Defence Helicopter Flying School, RAF Shawbury. The School is a tri-Service organisation consisting of civilian and military instructors that take the student from basic flying through to more advanced flying such as instrument flying, navigation, formation flying and captaincy. In service rotary-wing aircraft include: the Bell 212HP AH1,[18] the Eurocopter AS365N3 Dauphin II,[19] the Eurocopter Squirrel HT2,[20] the Westland Gazelle AH1,[21] the Westland Lynx AH9A[22] and the AgustaWestland Apache AH1.[23]
