In 2015, Robbie starred opposite Will Smith in romantic comedy-drama film Focus. In the film, she plays an inexperienced grifter learning the craft from Smith's character.[37] The film was released on 27 February 2015 to generally mixed reviews.[38] However, Robbie's performance was praised. Peter Travers of Rolling Stone wrote "Robbie is a wow and then some. The Aussie actress who made us sit up and take notice as Leonardo DiCaprio's wife in The Wolf of Wall Street shows a comic flair backed up with beauty and steel. Even when Focus fumbles, Robbie deals a winning hand."[39] Focus grossed over $150 million worldwide.[40][41] In 2015, Robbie received a nomination for the BAFTA Rising Star Award.[42] Also in 2015, Robbie played the leading role of Ann Burden in the film adaptation of Z for Zachariah with Chris Pine and Chiwetel Ejiofor, which was shot in New Zealand.[43][44] The film premiered on 24 January 2015 at the Sundance Film Festival [45] and had its theatrical release on 28 August 2015. The film received positive reviews.[46][47] Robbie's performance was praised by critics. Drew McWeeny of HitFix wrote "Robbie's work here establishes her as one of the very best actresses in her age range today".[48]
