Goa /ˈɡoʊ.ə/ ( listen)  is a state in India within the coastal region known as the Konkan, in Western India[4]. It is bounded by Maharashtra to the north and Karnataka to the east and south, with the Arabian Sea forming its Western coast. It is India's smallest state by area and the fourth smallest by population. Goa has the highest GDP per capita among all Indian states,[5] that is two and a half times that of the country.[6] It was ranked the 'best placed State' by the "Eleventh Finance Commission" for its infrastructure and ranked on top for the 'best quality of life' in India by the National Commission on Population based on the 12 Indicators.[6]
