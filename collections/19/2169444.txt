Despite pledging in April 2017 to veto a pro-abortion rights bill, which would have removed a clause that would make abortions illegal if Roe v. Wade were overturned and would remove restrictions on and expand taxpayer-funded abortions for those covered by Medicaid and state employee insurance,[125][119] Rauner reversed his pledge and signed the bill into law on September 28, 2017, something which earned him harsh criticism from conservative Republicans.[126]
