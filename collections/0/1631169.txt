Who uniting the Correctness of Judgement to the Fire of Genius, by the Melody & Power of his Numbers gave Sweetness to Sense, & Grace to Philosophy. He employ'd the pointed Brilliancy of Wit to chastise the Vices, and the Eloquence of Poetry to exalt the Virtues of human Nature; and being without a Rival in his own Age, imitated and translated, with a Spirit equal to the Originals, the best Poets of Antiquity.
