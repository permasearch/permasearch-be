Although policies favoring apartheid began earlier, the report provided endorsement for the idea that the maintenance of white superiority would require help from social institutions.[5][6] The report expressed fear about the loss of white racial pride, and in particular discussed the danger that the poor whites would not be able to resist the process of "Africanisation."[4] In seeking to prevent a class-based philosophy that would unite the poor of all races the report sought to increase race as opposed to class differences as the significant social category.[7]
