While the league struggled, the Grey Cup game itself retained its popularity and remained a national institution.[73] The strength of the contest allowed the league to endure its challenges.[74] The CFL survived into 1997 and was buoyed by a new television deal with The Sports Network which, along with the launch of its popular Friday Night Football program, has been credited with saving the league.[75] That year's Grey Cup, held in Edmonton and won by Toronto, drew nearly 22,000 more fans than the previous year.[48] The CFL restored its reputation over time, enjoying new popularity into the 2000s such that it no longer had to rely on an exciting Grey Cup final to achieve stability for the next season.[74]
