Baahubali: The Beginning on the first day of its release collected ₹75 crore (US$12 million) worldwide which was the highest opening ever for an Indian film until Kabali surpassed it in 2016 by earning ₹87.5 crore (US$14 million).[82] The film collected ₹15 crore (US$2.3 million) alone from United States on its first day.[83] First weekend collections stood around ₹162 crore (US$25 million) worldwide from all its versions, the third biggest ever for an Indian film.[84] The film grossed around ₹255 crore (US$40 million) worldwide in the first week of its release.[85] It became the first South Indian film to gross ₹300 crore (US$47 million) worldwide, reaching there in 9 days, and[86] subsequently grossed ₹401 crore (US$63 million) worldwide in 15 days.[87] And has successfully crossed ₹500 crore (US$78 million) mark in 24 days.[88] By the end of 50 days, the film grossed an approximate ₹595 crore (US$93 million) crore worldwide, based on estimates from International Business Times.[89] Overall collections of the film stood at ₹650 crore (US$100 million) as of today.[when?] Baahubali: The Beginning grossed ₹518 crore (US$81 million) in all languages in India alone,[90][91] and became the highest-grossing movie in India surpassing the gross of PK (2015) of ₹440 crore (US$69 million) from India.[92][93]
