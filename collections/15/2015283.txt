Bengal Chemicals & Pharmaceuticals Ltd. (BCPL), formerly Bengal Chemical & Pharmaceutical Works Ltd. (BCPW), is a Public Sector Undertaking (PSU). Established in Kolkata, West Bengal in 1901 by Prafulla Chandra Rây, it is India's first pharmaceutical company. The company manufactures such household Indian products as "Hospitol", "naphthalene balls", and "Phenyl".[1][2] Following over 6 decades of losses, BCPL posted a profit of ₹4 crore (US$620,000) in the 2016-17 fiscal year.
