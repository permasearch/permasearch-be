Other critics, such as Francis Fukuyama, note that "terrorism" is not an enemy, but a tactic; calling it a "war on terror", obscures differences between conflicts such as anti-occupation insurgents and international mujahideen. With a military presence in Iraq and Afghanistan and its associated collateral damage, Shirley Williams maintains this increases resentment and terrorist threats against the West.[274] There is also perceived U.S. hypocrisy,[275][276] media-induced hysteria,[277] and that differences in foreign and security policy have damaged America's reputation internationally.[278]
