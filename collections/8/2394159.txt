Over the first three days of the trial, whenever Charles was asked to plead, he refused,[275] stating his objection with the words: "I would know by what power I am called hither, by what lawful authority...?"[276] He claimed that no court had jurisdiction over a monarch,[264] that his own authority to rule had been given to him by God and by the traditional laws of England, and that the power wielded by those trying him was only that of force of arms.
