Sri Lanka has 45 estuaries and 40 lagoons.[167] Sri Lanka's mangrove ecosystem spans over 7,000 hectares and played a vital role in buffering the force of the waves in the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami.[169] The island is rich in minerals such as ilmenite, feldspar, graphite, silica, kaolin, mica and thorium.[170][171] Existence of petroleum and gas in the Gulf of Mannar has also been confirmed and the extraction of recoverable quantities is underway.[172]
