A 2007 survey of undergraduates also suggests that 62.8% are sexually active, while 6.2% identify as LGBTQ.[13][149] Discrimination can be an issue on campus, and three-fourths of a 2009 survey considered homophobia a campus problem.[150] Newsweek, however, rated Georgetown among its top "Gay-Friendly Schools" in 2010.[151] A survey by the school in 2016 showed that 31% of females undergraduates reported experiencing unwanted sexual contact, and 86% of LGBTQ students reported some form of sexual harassment at the college.[152] In 2011, College Magazine ranked Georgetown as the tenth most hipster U.S. college,[153] while People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals considered it the third most vegan friendly small U.S. school.[154]
