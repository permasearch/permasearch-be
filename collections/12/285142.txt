The only part of the former Soviet Union where admiration for Stalin has remained consistently widespread is Georgia.[900] Many Georgians resent criticism of Stalin, the most famous figure from their nation's modern history;[894] a 2013 survey by Tbilisi University found 45% of Georgians expressing "a positive attitude" to him.[901] In a 2012 opinion survey commissioned by the Carnegie Endowment, 38% of Armenians agreed with the statement, "Our people will always have need of a leader like Stalin, who will come and restore order."[902][903]
