Early in 1978, the term was used in a working group finding presented to the Committee of Reactive Disorders.[217] The condition was added to the DSM-III, which was being developed in the 1980s, as posttraumatic stress disorder.[215][217] In the DSM-IV, the spelling "posttraumatic stress disorder" is used, while in the ICD-10, the spelling is "post-traumatic stress disorder".[218]
