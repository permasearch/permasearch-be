Several means were employed to maintain secrecy. All scripts contained a watermark that ensured they could not be photocopied, and copies of scripts used on the set were kept in a safe.[71] Only Chibnall and four other individuals knew at the start of production who the killer would be.[71] Even ITV Director of Television Peter Fincham was not told the killer's identity until the first few days of March 2013.[71] All cast and crew were required to sign documents in which they agreed not to reveal the killer's identity.[41]
