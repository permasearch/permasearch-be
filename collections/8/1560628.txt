Barrani was specific in his recommendation that the "sons of Mohamed" [i.e. Ashrafs] "be given a higher social status than the low-born [i.e. Ajlaf].[130] His most significant contribution in the fatwa was his analysis of the castes with respect to Islam.[130] His assertion was that castes would be mandated through state laws or "Zawabi" and would carry precedence over Sharia law whenever they were in conflict.[130] Every act which is "contaminated with meanness and based on ignominity, comes elegantly [from the Ajlaf]".[130] He sought appropriate religious sanction to that effect.[121] Barrani also developed an elaborate system of promotion and demotion of imperial officers ("Wazirs") that was primarily on the basis of their caste.[130]
