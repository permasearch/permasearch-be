Assessing Russian Activities and Intentions in Recent US Elections (also abbreviated as Russian Activities and Intentions in Recent US Elections,[1] Assessing Russian Activities and Intentions in Recent Elections[2] or simply Assessing Russian Activities and Intentions[3]) was a report issued by the United States Intelligence Community that made an assessment of the extent and basis of Russia's interference in United States' elections in 2016. Published on January 6, 2017, by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence, the report includes an appraisal by the National Security Agency, the Central Intelligence Agency, and the Federal Bureau of Investigation of the type and breadth of action undertaken by Russia and affiliated elements during the elections. The report examines Russia's utilization of cyberspace such as hacking and the use of internet trolls and bots, and an intensive media campaign to influence public opinion in the United States. Additionally, it analyzes Russia's intentions and motivations in regards to the influence campaign. Issued in two forms, a classified version and a declassified version, the report drew its conclusions based on highly classified intelligence, an understanding of past Russian actions, and sensitive sources and methods.
