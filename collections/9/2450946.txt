North Carolina State University offers numerous non-varsity and club level sports throughout the year. This includes, but is not limited to, hockey, lacrosse, basketball, baseball, soccer, rugby, swimming, cheerleading, sailing, ultimate frisbee and much more. The North Carolina State University Men's Rugby Football Club was founded in 1965.[7] NC State plays college rugby in the Atlantic Coast Rugby League against its traditional ACC rivals. The NC State rugby team is led by head coach Jim Latham.[8] The Wolfpack play their home games at the Upper Method Road Field. NC State won the Atlantic Coast Invitational 7s tournament in 2010 and 2011.[9] The Wolfpack finished 13th at the 2011 USA Rugby Sevens Collegiate National Championships. NC State finished 12th at the 2012 Collegiate Rugby Championship, a tournament broadcast live on NBC from PPL Park in Philadelphia. NC State scored a notable upset against #7 ranked Davenport to reach the finals of the 2012 ACI 7s tournament in Blacksburg, only to lose in the final to host Virginia Tech.[10]
