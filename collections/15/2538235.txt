Following his seminary education, Hoffman began work with the publishing branch of the Evangelical Association in Cleveland, Ohio.[2] After serving in this position for 11 years, Hoffman held several pastoral positions in the midwest. He pastored churches in both Cleveland and Grafton, Ohio, in the 1880s; moved to Benton Harbor, Michigan and the First Presbyterian Church in the mid-1890s; and finished his ministry in Cabery, Illinois from 1911-22.[3] Hoffman died in 1929 in Chicago, Illinois, and is buried there in Oak Woods Cemetery.[4]
