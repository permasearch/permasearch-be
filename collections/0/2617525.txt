People with high scores on a psychopathy rating scale are more likely to engage in bullying, crime and drug use than other people.[78] Hare and Babiak noted that about 29 per cent of corporate psychopaths are also bullies.[79] Other research has also shown that people with high scores on a psychopathy rating scale were more likely to engage in bullying, again indicating that psychopaths tend to be bullies in the workplace.[78]
