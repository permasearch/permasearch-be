Juliane Okot Bitek's book of poetry, 100 Days (University of Alberta Press, 2016) uses the Rwandan genocide as a framework within which to explore "the senseless loss of life and of innocence" as well as "her own family's experience of displacement under the regime of Idi Amin"[283] Juliane Okot Bitek is the daughter of Ugandan poet Okot p'Bitek.
