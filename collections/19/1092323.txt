In 2005, the World Bank listed Kazakhstan as a corruption hotspot, on a par with Angola, Bolivia, Kenya, Libya and Pakistan.[153] In 2012, Kazakhstan ranked low in an index of the least corrupt countries[154] and the World Economic Forum listed corruption as the biggest problem in doing business in the country.[154] A 2017 OECD report on Kazakhstan indicated that Kazakhstan has reformed laws with regard to the civil service, judiciary, instruments to prevent corruption, access to information, and prosecuting corruption.[155]
