The game was first unveiled at E3 2014 during Microsoft's pre-show press conference at the Galen Center;[5] E3 was also the first time a number of Moon Studios employees actually met face-to-face.[7] Microsoft's Yusuf Mehdi, in charge of marketing for Xbox One, stated that they considered opening the conference with Ori, but instead chose Call of Duty: Advanced Warfare.[5] During E3, attendees waited in long lines to play a demo version of the game, often waiting in queues 7-8 people deep for each of the four consoles featuring the game.[5]
