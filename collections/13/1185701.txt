On June 7, Beltrán became the 38th player all time to record 1,000 career extra base hits.[119] He was selected to his ninth All-Star Game at Petco Park in San Diego.[120] In a 5−3 loss to the Boston Red Sox on July 15, he became the 55th player, and fourth switch-hitter, to reach 1,500 RBIs for his career.[121] In 99 games with the Yankees, Beltrán batted .304 with 22 home runs and 64 RBIs, leading the Yankees in all categories.[112]
