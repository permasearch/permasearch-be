Article IX covered procedures for regency, which should be taken up jointly by the council of the Guardians, headed by the Queen, or in her absence by the Primate.[85][101] Article X stressed the importance of education of royal children and tasked the Commission of National Education with this responsibility.[101] The last article of the constitution, Article XI, concerned the national standing army.[85] Said army was defined as a "defensive force" dedicated "solely to the nation's defense."[85] The army was to be increased in strength to 100,000 men.[102]
