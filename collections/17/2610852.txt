According to review aggregator Metacritic, the game received an average review score of 96/100 for Xbox 360 and Microsoft Windows, and 94/100 for PlayStation 3.[82][83][84] As of July 2013[update], it is one of the highest-rated games on Metacritic, tied with a number of other games for the third-highest aggregate score.[102] GameSpy praised BioShock's "inescapable atmosphere",[103] and Official Xbox Magazine lauded its "inconceivably great plot" and "stunning soundtrack and audio effects."[93] The gameplay and combat system have been praised for being smooth and open-ended,[16][90] and elements of the graphics, such as the water, were praised for their quality.[21] It has been noted that the combination of the game's elements "straddles so many entertainment art forms so expertly that it's the best demonstration yet how flexible this medium can be. It's no longer just another shooter wrapped up in a pretty game engine, but a story that exists and unfolds inside the most convincing and elaborate and artistic game world ever conceived."[89]
