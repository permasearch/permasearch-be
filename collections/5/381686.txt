In its first decades, the IWW created more than 900 unions located in more than 350 cities and towns in 38 states and territories of the United States and 5 Canadian provinces.[24] Throughout the country, there were 90 newspapers and periodicals affiliated with the IWW, published in 19 different languages. Members of the IWW were active throughout the country and were involved in the Seattle General Strike,[25] were arrested or killed in the Everett Massacre,[26] organized among Mexican workers in the Southwest,[27] became a largest and powerful longshoremen's union in Philadelphia,[28] and more.
