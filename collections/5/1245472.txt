The segment of I-75 through the Downriver suburbs of Detroit between the US 24 (Telegraph Road) connector and M-39 (Southfield Highway) was completed on December 28, 1966.[88] The same year, I-75 was scheduled to open southward from 11 Mile Road to M-102 (8 Mile Road).[89] In 1967, two segments of freeway opened. One was from Kawkawlin to Standish in October,[90] and the other through Detroit extended I-75 along the Fisher Freeway in December.[91] The first section of M-76 freeway from Standish northwesterly to Alger was scheduled to open in July 1968.[92] A one-mile (1.6 km) section of the Chrysler Freeway through Detroit opened on December 19, 1968, and the remainder was scheduled to open on January 10, 1969.[93]
