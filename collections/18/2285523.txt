Saving Christmas is a faith-based Christmas comedy film starring Kirk Cameron, who plays a fictionalized version of himself attempting to convince his brother-in-law (played by the film's director, Darren Doane) that Christmas is still a Christian holiday. On Rotten Tomatoes, the film received a rating of 0%, based on 13 reviews, with an average rating of 1.7/10.[470] Critic David Keyes described Saving Christmas as "The worst holiday movie ever made",[470] Billings Gazette selected the film as the worst Christmas movie of all time,[471] and Will Nicol of Digital Trends included it on his list of the ten worst movies ever made.[472] Christy Lemire picked Saving Christmas as the worst film she has ever reviewed.[473] In response to the criticism, Cameron asked fans to give the film positive reviews on Rotten Tomatoes.[474] This resulted in a backlash in which Internet users traveled to the Rotten Tomatoes page and condemned the film.[475] Three weeks after its release, Saving Christmas became the lowest-rated film on IMDb's bottom 100 list. Cameron blamed the low rating on a Reddit campaign by "haters and atheists" to purposely lower the film's ratings.[476] The film won four awards, including Worst Picture, at the 35th Golden Raspberry Awards.[477]
