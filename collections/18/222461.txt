They regard themselves as an exclusive and elite community, membership of which is restricted to those who have the "insight" to recognise the importance of Haile Selassie.[291] Rastas often regard themselves as being the "enlightened ones" who have "seen the light".[292] Many see no point in establishing good relations with non-Rastas, believing that the latter will never accept Rastafari doctrine as truth.[293] English Rastas have for instance expressed criticism of black Britons who have not embraced the religion, stating that they have been "brainwashed", "misguided by European Christianity", and "blinded by Babylon".[294]
