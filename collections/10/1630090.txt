Bayern is an exception in professional football, having generated profits for 25 years in a row.[134] Other clubs often report losses, realizing transfers via loans, whereas Bayern always uses current assets. In the 2018 edition of the Deloitte Football Money League Bayern had the fourth highest revenue in club football, generating revenue of €587.8 million. Bayern differs from other European top clubs in their income composition. The top 20 European football clubs had an average of 45% of their revenues from broadcasting rights. Bayern earned only 25% of their revenue that way. Bayern have the highest commercial revenue in the 2018 Deloitte Football Money League. Bayern's commercial revenue is at €343.4 million (58% of their total revenue). In contrast, Bayern's Matchday revenue is lacking behind the top clubs at €97.7 million (17% of their total revenue).[135]
