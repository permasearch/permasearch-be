On the week ending March 7, 2009, "The Climb" debuted at number 48 on Billboard's Hot Country Songs. It was, incidentally, the same week that "Back to Tennessee", by Cyrus's father, Billy Ray Cyrus, debuted at number 59, the first time a father and daughter had separate charting songs on the chart since Johnny and Rosanne Cash charted in 1990 with "Silver Stallion" and "One Step over the Line", respectively.[27] "The Climb" eventually peaked at number 25 on the country-genre chart.[28] For the week ending March 21, 2009, the song debuted at number six on the Billboard Hot 100 due to sales of 166,000 digital downloads,[29] thus making "The Climb" Cyrus's highest-charting effort at the time, surpassing her previous best-charting effort "7 Things", which peaked at number nine in July 2008.[29] For the week ending May 2, 2009, the song reached its peak at number four on the Hot 100.[30] "The Climb" also peaked at number seven on the Pop 100 chart, 42 on Hot Christian Songs, and at number one for 15 consecutive weeks on Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks.[28] Cyrus became the youngest artist to top Adult Contemporary since LeAnn Rimes entered the chart in 1997 with "How Do I Live".[31] According to Billboard, "The Climb" was the eighth best selling digital single of 2009.[32] The single was certified triple platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) for the sales of over two million copies.[33] As of January 2014, "The Climb" has sold 3,709,000 copies in the United States.[34] In Canada, "The Climb" entered at number 17 and eventually peaked at number five.[35] The single was certified triple platinum by Music Canada for the sales of 240,000 digital downloads.[36]
