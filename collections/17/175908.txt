Due to the mixed reception and low ratings of the previous year's ceremony, producers David Hill and Reginald Hudlin declined to helm the Oscar production. They were replaced by Michael De Luca and Jennifer Todd as producers.[39][40] Actor and comedian Chris Rock told Variety regarding if he would return to host, "someone else will do it."[41] On December 5, 2016, it was announced that Jimmy Kimmel would host the ceremony.[42] Kimmel expressed that it was truly an honor and a thrill to be asked to host Academy Awards, commenting "Mike and Jennifer have an excellent plan and their enthusiasm is infectious. I am honored to have been chosen to host the 89th and final Oscars."[43]
