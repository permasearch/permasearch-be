Experimental musician Greg Davis and jazz singer-songwriter[142] Chris Weisman recorded the track for their 2010 album Northern Songs,[143] a project that The Village Voice described as blending "Beatlefolk" with "gongs, field recordings, and generally orchestrated nirvana".[144] The Flaming Lips performed "It's All Too Much" at the George Fest tribute concert in September 2014,[145] with special guest Gingger Shankar playing violin.[146] Consequence of Sound's reviewer described it as "the most sonically pleasing song of the night".[147]
