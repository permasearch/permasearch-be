The production returned to Minnesota, where it was plagued by a lack of snow.[21] To solve this problem, the filmmakers assembled a special effects team to create a combination of real snow and fake synthetic snow that was made from shaved ice.[21]:2 The home of Lou Chambers and his wife Nancy was filmed in an abandoned house in Delano,[20] which cinematographer Alar Kivilo described as "a very difficult [filming] location with very low ceilings and no heating".[21] Brandenstein and the art department were tasked with designing the set inside the home.[21][22]
