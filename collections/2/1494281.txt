Johnson & Johnson has set several positive goals to keep the company environmentally friendly and was ranked third among the United States's largest companies in Newsweek's "Green Rankings".[76] Some examples are the reduction in water use, waste, and energy use and an increased level of transparency.[77] Johnson & Johnson agreed to change its packaging of plastic bottles used in the manufacturing process, switching their packaging of liquids to non-polycarbonate containers.[78] The corporation is working with the Climate Northwest Initiative and the EPA National Environmental Performance Track program.[79] As a member of the national Green Power Partnership, Johnson & Johnson operates the largest solar power generator in Pennsylvania at its site in Spring House, PA.[80]
