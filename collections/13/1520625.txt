Already Castaneda noted the typical relations of two different plains people relying heavily on the same food source, "they ... are enemies of each other."[38] The bison hunting resulted in loss of land for at number of Indian nations. Indirectly it often disturbed the rhythm of a tribal life, caused economic losses, hardship and damaged tribal autonomy. As long as bison hunting went on, intertribal warfare was omnipresent.[39][40]
