The total state product in 2015 was $318,600 million.[96] Per capita personal income in 2010 was $51 940, ranking Colorado 11th in the nation.[97] The state's economy broadened from its mid-19th-century roots in mining when irrigated agriculture developed, and by the late 19th century, raising livestock had become important. Early industry was based on the extraction and processing of minerals and agricultural products. Current agricultural products are cattle, wheat, dairy products, corn, and hay.
