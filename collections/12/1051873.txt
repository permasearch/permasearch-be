Fondly called the "father of African literature",[146] Achebe died after a short illness on 21 March 2013 in Boston, United States. An unidentified source close to the family said that he was ill for a while and had been hospitalised in the city.[147][148] Penguin publishing director Simon Winder said: "...we are all desolate to hear of his death."[149] The New York Times described him in his obituary as "one of Africa's most widely read novelists and one of the continent's towering men of letters".[150] The BBC wrote that he was "revered throughout the world for his depiction of life in Africa".[151] He was laid to rest in his hometown in Ogidi, Anambra State.[152]
