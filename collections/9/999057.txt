Shares of GameStop stock fell 16% throughout 2016.[42]  On February 28, 2017, shares dropped an additional 8% following Microsoft's announcement of its Xbox Game Pass service. [43]  Following these reports, GameStop announced it would close over 150 stores in 2017 and expand its nongaming business.[44] On the same day, however, GameStop said it planned to open 65 new Technology Brand stores and 35 Collectibles stores due to a 44% and 28% increase in sales, respectively.[45]  GameStop expects a continued drop in operating income between 3% and 10% in 2017.[46]
