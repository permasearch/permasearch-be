Another norovirus outbreak was confirmed to have occurred in August 2015 at a Simi Valley, California location in which 80 customers and 18 employees reported becoming ill.[174][175] Ventura County health inspectors found various health violations during two inspections following the outbreak report.[176] Despite those violations, the county health officials did not close the restaurant and allowed it to continue to operate.[174] In a January 2016 article, The New York Times reported that the number of victims involved in the Simi Valley norovirus outbreak was actually 207, twice the number that was reported earlier.[177]
