Augustus made Judea a Roman province in 6 CE, deposing the last Jewish king, Herod Archelaus and appointing a Roman governor. There was a small revolt against Roman taxation led by Judas of Galilee and over the next decades tensions grew between the Greco-Roman and Judean population centered on attempts to place effigies of the Emperor Caligula in Synagogues and in the Jewish temple.[40][41]
