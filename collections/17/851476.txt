Touchstone Pictures opted to move the release date up a week, from the original October 27, to October 20, 2006.[34] The film earned $14.8 million on opening weekend in the United States, debuting at #1. It grossed $109 million, including $53 million from the United States.[3] The film received nominations for the Academy Award for Best Art Direction (Nathan Crowley and Julie Ochipinti) and the Academy Award for Best Cinematography (Wally Pfister),[35] as well as a nomination for the Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation, Long Form in 2007.[36] Along with The Illusionist and Scoop (also starring Jackman and Johansson), The Prestige was one of three films in 2006 to explore the world of stage magicians.
