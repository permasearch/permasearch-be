Martin Luther King, Jr. sang at the gala as part of a children's choir of his father's church, Ebenezer Baptist.[39] The boys dressed as pickaninnies and the girls wore "Aunt Jemima"-style bandanas, dress seen by many blacks as humiliating.[40][41] John Wesley Dobbs tried to dissuade Rev. King, Sr. from participating at the whites-only event, and Rev. King, Sr. was harshly criticized in the black community.
