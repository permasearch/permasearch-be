Weis and the Irish went into the 2006 season with a No. 2 preseason ranking in the ESPN/Coaches Poll.[432] They finished the regular season with a 10–2 record,[433] losing only to Michigan[434] and USC.[435] Notre Dame accepted a bid to the 2007 Sugar Bowl, losing to LSU 41–14.[436] This marked their ninth consecutive post-season loss, the longest drought in NCAA history.[437] As a result, Notre Dame dropped to No. 17 in the final rankings.[438][439]
