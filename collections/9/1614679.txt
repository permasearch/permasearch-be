The arrival of Spaniards in Jamaica began in 1494, with the arrival of Christopher Columbus to the island in search of gold (as in Cuba and the Spanish, where he had reported the existence of the island, called it Xaymaca, who wants say, in the indigenous language, "place of gold blessed"), but then discovered that this was nonexistent in island. Columbus named the island as Santiago and used it as a mini-state for family. addition, he and his sailors founded some places, such as Santa Gloria (now Bay St. Anne), named by Columbus, as it was the first that was sighted when they came to Jamaica.
