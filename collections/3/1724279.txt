According to the database Ethnologue (published by the Summer Institute of Linguistics), up to two million people worldwide, to varying degrees, speak Esperanto,[11] including about 1000 to 2000 native speakers who learned Esperanto from birth.[1] The World Esperanto Association has more than 5500 members in 120[12] countries. Its usage is highest in Europe, East Asia, and South America.[13] lernu! is one of the most popular online learning platforms for Esperanto and reported 150,000 registered users in 2013, and sees between 150,000 and 200,000 visitors each month.[14] With about 242,000 articles, Esperanto Wikipedia is the 32nd-largest Wikipedia as measured by the number of articles,[15] and is the largest Wikipedia in a constructed language.[16] On 22 February 2012, Google Translate added Esperanto as its 64th language.[17] On 28 May 2015, the language learning platform Duolingo launched an Esperanto course for English speakers.[18] On 26 October 2016, the course for Spanish speakers appeared on the same platform.[19] As of 27 May 2017[update], over one million users have started to learn Esperanto on Duolingo.[20]
