The Moti Masjid (Hindustani: موتی مسجد, मोती मस्जिद) is a white marble mosque inside the Red Fort complex in Delhi, India. The name translates into English as "Pearl Mosque."[1] Located to the west of the Hammam and close to the Diwan-e-Khas, it was built by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb from 1659-1660.
