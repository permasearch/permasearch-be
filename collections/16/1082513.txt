The original screenwriter for the film was Len Deighton, but he was replaced because of his slow progress. Two of the writers from Dr. No, Richard Maibaum and Johanna Harwood, were brought in, with Maibaum being given the sole writing credit and Harwood being credited for "adaptation".[41] From Russia with Love is the first Bond film in the series with John Barry as the primary soundtrack composer,[42] although Lionel Bart wrote the title song "From Russia with Love", sung by Matt Monro.[43] Principal photography began on 1 April 1963[44] and concluded on 23 August.[45] Filming took place in Turkey, Pinewood Studios and Venice, with Scotland and Switzerland doubling for the Orient Express journey through Eastern Europe.[46]
