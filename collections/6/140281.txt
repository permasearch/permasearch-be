SNAP benefits cost $70.9 billion in fiscal year 2016 and supplied roughly 44.2 million Americans with an average of $125.51 for each person per month in food assistance. Beneficiaries and costs increased sharply with the Great Recession, peaked in 2013 and have declined through 2016 as the economy recovered.[2] It is the largest nutrition program of the fifteen administered by FNS and is a component of the federal social safety net for low-income Americans.[3]
