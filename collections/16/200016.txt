Washington cities may establish Municipal Courts.  [4] [5]  Municipal Courts are courts of limited jurisdiction like state District Courts, but Municipal Courts may not hear civil lawsuits.  [6] A Municipal Court may only consider and has exclusive jurisdiction over non-criminal traffic citations, as well as misdemeanor and gross misdemeanor crimes that occur within a city's boundary.[7] Municipal Courts conduct trials and other attendant hearings. [8]   Municipal Court judges are elected  or appointed by mayors or city councils and serve four-year terms.
