NYCO Renaissance presented Puccini's Tosca – using Adolfo Hohenstein's stage and costume design from the opera's premiere in 1900 in Rome – in January 2016 at the 1,100-seat Rose Theater.[65][65] It had two separate casts, including tenor James Valenti and soprano Latonia Moore, and the least expensive balcony seats were $25.[10][65][66][67] Tosca had been, in 1944, the first opera performed by the NYCO.[8][61][65]
