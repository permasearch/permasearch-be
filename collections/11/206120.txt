On December 30, 2014, the University announced the hiring of Jim Harbaugh as the team's 20th head coach. Harbaugh, who was starting quarterback in the early 1980s under Bo Schembechler, had most recently served as head coach of the San Francisco 49ers. In his first season, Harbaugh led Michigan to a 10–3 record, including a 41–7 win over the Florida Gators in the 2016 Citrus Bowl.[86] The squad achieved an identical 10-3 record during the 2016 season, which ended with a 33-32 loss to Florida State in the Orange Bowl on December 30, 2016.
