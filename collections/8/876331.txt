Since the Antiquities Act was first signed into effect under the 26th President of the United States, all but four sitting presidents, Richard Nixon, Gerald Ford, Ronald Reagan, and George H.W. Bush have chosen to enlarge or dedicate new national monuments.[2]  President Obama in his last term in office erected the most monuments, under the Antiquities Act, than any President before him with 26. The President to have designated the highest number of monuments before President Obama was President Theodore Roosevelt with 18 monuments.[3]
