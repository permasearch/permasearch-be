Ahead of the general election, crowdfunding groups such as More United and Open Britain were formed to promote candidates of similar views standing for election, and a "progressive alliance" was proposed.[77][78][79][80] Former UKIP donor Arron Banks suggested a "patriotic alliance" movement.[81] Tactical voting to keep the Conservatives out of government was suggested on social media.[82][83] Gina Miller, who took the government to court over Article 50, set out plans to tour marginal constituencies in support of pro-EU candidates.[84]
