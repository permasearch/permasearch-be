In the late 14th century, mentions the memoir of Tughlaq dynasty's Sultan Firoz Shah Tughlaq, his predecessor taxed all Hindus but had exempted all Hindu Brahmins from jizya; Firoz Shah extended it over all Hindus.[197][198] He also announced that any Hindus who converted to Islam would become exempt from taxes and jizya as well as receive gifts from him.[197][199] On those who chose to remain Hindus, he raised jizya tax rate.[197]
