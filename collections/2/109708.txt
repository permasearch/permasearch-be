ISIS captured Qaryatayn city from the government on 5 August 2015.[269] Australia joined the bombing of ISIL in Syria in mid September, an extension of their efforts in Iraq for the last year.[270] On 2 August, U.S. officials informed Reuters that the United States had decided to "allow air strikes to help defend against any attack on the U.S.-trained Syrian rebels, even if the attackers come from forces loyal to Syrian President Bashar al-Assad." The following day the Pentagon announced that it would begin flying its first unmanned, armed drone missions in Syria.[271]
