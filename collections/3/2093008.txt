A protective effect of caffeine against Alzheimer's disease and dementia is possible but the evidence is inconclusive.[95][96][97] It may protect people from liver cirrhosis.[98] Caffeine may lessen the severity of acute mountain sickness if taken a few hours prior to attaining a high altitude.[99] One meta analysis has found that caffeine consumption is associated with a reduced risk of type 2 diabetes.[100] Two meta analyses have reported that caffeine consumption is associated with a linear reduction in risk for parkinson's disease.[101][16] Caffeine consumption may be associated with reduced risk of depression,[102] although conflicting results have been reported.[103]
