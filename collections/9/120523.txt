As of 2009, 62% of illegal immigrants in the United States originate from Mexico,[59] but by 2014, they made up 52% of illegal immigrants.[60] Commonly those who enter the United States illegally are smuggled in by individuals referred to as "coyotes".[61] In 2005, according to the World Bank, Mexico received US$18.1 billion in remittances from individuals in the United States.[62] The number of illegal immigrants was at its highest in 2007, at 12.2 million, and has since dropped to 11.1 million in 2014.[60] In response to this and the trafficking of illegal drugs the United States has built a barrier on its border with Mexico.[63]
