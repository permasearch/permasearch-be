The hill tribe people in Indochina were "hunted incessantly and carried off as slaves by the Siamese (Thai), the Anamites (Vietnamese), and the Cambodians".[227] A Siamese military campaign in Laos in 1876 was described by a British observer as having been "transformed into slave-hunting raids on a large scale".[228] The census, taken in 1879, showed that 6% of the population in the Malay sultanate of Perak were slaves.[229] Enslaved people made up about two-thirds of the population in part of North Borneo in the 1880s.[229]
