Slow-simmered stews with various ingredients are another common food staple in Ivory Coast.[97] Kedjenou is a dish consisting of chicken and vegetables that are slow-cooked in a sealed pot with little or no added liquid, which concentrates the flavors of the chicken and vegetables and tenderizes the chicken.[97] It is usually cooked in a pottery jar called a canary, over a slight fire, or cooked in an oven.[97] Bangui is a local palm wine.
