The first post office opened in 1854.[15] The first school, Schmermerhorn School, was opened in 1855 (and eventually became Lincoln School), Encinal School was opened in 1860 (and closed in 1980). The San Francisco and Alameda Railroad opened the Encinal station in 1864.[15] The Encinal area was also known as Fasskings Station in honor of Frederick Louis Fassking.[15] Encinal's own post office opened in 1876, was renamed West End in 1877, and closed in 1891.[15] The West End area was originally called Bowman's Point in honor of Charles G. Bowman, an early settler.[15]
