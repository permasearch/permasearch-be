In November 2006, Woods announced his intention to begin designing golf courses around the world through a new company, Tiger Woods Design.[219] A month later, he announced that the company's first course would be in Dubai as part of a 25.3 million-square-foot development, The Tiger Woods Dubai.[220] The Al Ruwaya Golf Course was initially expected to finish construction in 2009.[220] As of February 2010, only seven holes had been completed; in April 2011, The New York Times reported that the project had been shelved permanently.[221][222] In 2013, the partnership between Tiger Woods Design and Dubai Holding was dissolved.[223]
