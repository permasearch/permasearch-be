Firmino said that it was his "dream" to play in the national team though he had no "contact with national team manager Dunga".[78] On 23 October 2014, Firmino received his first call up to Brazil national football team for the friendly matches against Turkey and Austria. He commented, "I'm very happy about the nomination and would especially like to thank the team".[79] He debuted in a 4–0 win over Turkey on 12 November, replacing fellow debutant Luiz Adriano for the last 17 minutes.[80] Firmino scored his first goal  six days later in the latter match, a 2–1 away win.[81]
