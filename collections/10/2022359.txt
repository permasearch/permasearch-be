After the release of Far from Close, Hinder was offered record deals by Atlantic Records, Roadrunner Records, and Universal Records, eventually signing with Universal Records in 2005;[5] later that year, their major-label debut album, Extreme Behavior, was released.[7] The album was produced by Brian Howes, who, along with Cody Hanson and Austin Winkler, wrote most of the material on the album.[8] The album was engineered by Mike Fraser and Jay Van Poederooyen.[9] The album reached triple-platinum certification for sales,[7][10]
