The Front Veranda (1986), a lithograph by Susan Dorothea White in the National Gallery of Australia,[248] exemplifies worldwide awareness of the event. Heavy Water: A Film for Chernobyl was released by Seventh Art in 2006 to commemorate the disaster through poetry and first-hand accounts.[249] The film secured the Best Short Documentary at Cinequest Film Festival as well as the Rhode Island "best score" award[250] along with a screening at Tate Modern.[251]
