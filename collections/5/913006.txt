California has been the state leading hybrid sales in the U.S. with 55,553 vehicles sold in 2009,[179] 74,932 in 2008,[137] and 91,417 in 2007.[138] In 2009 it was followed by New York (15,438) and Florida (14,949).[179] In terms of new hybrids sold per capita, the District of Columbia was the leader in 2009 with 3.79 hybrids per 1000 residents, followed by California (1.54) and Washington (1.53).[179] The top 5 U. S. metropolitan area markets for sales of hybrid electric vehicles in 2009 were Los Angeles (26,677), New York (21,193), San Francisco (15,799), Washington, D.C. (11,595), and Chicago (8,990).[179]
