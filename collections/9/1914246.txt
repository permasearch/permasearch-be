Ronaldo played for 15 minutes in a match that ended with a Brazilian victory with a goal from Fred.[73] Fred celebrated his goal with Ronaldo's famous 'finger wag' celebration along with his Brazilian teammates. Ronaldo was introduced after 30 minutes, partnering new star striker Neymar in attack, and had three shots on target which were saved by the Romanian goalkeeper, Ciprian Tătărușanu.[74] After the first half ended, Ronaldo made a farewell speech to the crowd.[74] Ronaldo retired from international football as the second highest goalscorer for Brazil, behind only Pelé, with 62 goals in 98 appearances.[75]
