Jordanes wrote that Venethi, Sclavenes and Antes were ethnonyms referring to the same group.[59] His claim was accepted more than a millennium later by Wawrzyniec Surowiecki, Pavel Jozef Šafárik and other historians,[60] who searched the Slavic Urheimat in the lands where the Venethi (a people named in Tacitus's Germania)[61] lived during the last decades of the first century AD.[62] Pliny the Elder wrote that the territory extending from the Vistula to Aeningia (probably Feningia, or Finland), was inhabited by the Sarmati, Wends, Scirii and Hirri.[63]
