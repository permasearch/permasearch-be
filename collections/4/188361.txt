The state is a leader in renewable energy commercialization; it produces the most wind power in the nation.[182][185] In 2014, 10.6% of the electricity consumed in Texas came from wind turbines.[186] The Roscoe Wind Farm in Roscoe, Texas, is one of the world's largest wind farms with a 781.5 megawatt (MW) capacity.[187] The Energy Information Administration states the state's large agriculture and forestry industries could give Texas an enormous amount biomass for use in biofuels. The state also has the highest solar power potential for development in the nation.[182]
