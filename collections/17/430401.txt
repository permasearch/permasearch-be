Iacocca wanted the new car, which returned the Mustang to its 1964 predecessor in size, shape, and overall styling,[38] to be finished to a high standard, saying it should be "a little jewel."[39] Not only was it smaller than the original car, but it was also heavier, owing to the addition of equipment needed to meet new U.S. emission and safety regulations. Performance was reduced, and despite the car's new handling and engineering features the galloping mustang emblem "became a less muscular steed that seemed to be cantering.[40]
