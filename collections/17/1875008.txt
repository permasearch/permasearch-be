Churchill's was a more cautious approach ("the unionist position") to European integration than was the continental approach that was known as "the federalist position".[18] The Federalists advocated full integration with a constitution, while the Unionist United Europe Movement advocated a consultative body, and the Federalists prevailed at the Congress of Europe.[18] The primary accomplishment of the Congress of Europe was the European Court of Human Rights, which predates the European Union.[19]
