It is a more secure replacement for the paper IMM 1000 form previously used to identify permanent residents, and was first proposed in 2001 (originally named the "Maple Leaf card") as part of the Canadian government's overhaul of immigration and security laws following the September 11 attacks in the United States.[1] After the 2001 passage of the new Immigration and Refugee Protection Act, the first PR cards were distributed on 28 June 2002.
