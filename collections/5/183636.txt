Although British Prime Minister Gordon Brown stated his support for the United States on assuming office in 2007,[165] he appointed ministers to the Foreign Office who had been critical of aspects of the relationship or of recent US policy.[166][167] A Whitehall source said: 'It will be more businesslike now, with less emphasis on the meeting of personal visions you had with Bush and Blair.'[168] British policy was that the relationship with the United States remained the United Kingdom's 'most important bilateral relationship'.[169]
