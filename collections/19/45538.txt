The plan was approved by Johnson and marked a profound departure from the previous administration's insistence that the government of South Vietnam was responsible for defeating the guerrillas. Westmoreland predicted victory by the end of 1967.[184] Johnson did not, however, communicate this change in strategy to the media. Instead he emphasized continuity.[185] The change in U.S. policy depended on matching the North Vietnamese and the Viet Cong in a contest of attrition and morale. The opponents were locked in a cycle of escalation.[186] The idea that the government of South Vietnam could manage its own affairs was shelved.[186]
