All these factors meant that most women and children were simply left in jail.[21] Some magistrates supported a proposal to release women who could not be transported, but this solution was considered absurd: this caused the Lords Justices to order that no distinction be made between men and women.[38] Women were sent to the Leeward Islands, the only colony that accepted them, and the government had to pay to send them overseas.[39] In 1696 Jamaica refused to welcome a group of prisoners because most of them were women; Barbados similarly accepted convicts but not "women, children nor other infirm persons".[40]
