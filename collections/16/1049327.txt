The Fighting Irish would go 10–3 in 1991.[282] After defeating Indiana to open the season,[283] the Irish lost to #4 Michigan by a score of 24–14.[284] The Irish won their next seven, defeating Michigan State,[285] Purdue,[286] Stanford,[287] #12 Pittsburgh,[288] Air Force,[289] USC[290] and Navy.[291] The Irish would suffer a defeat to #13 Tennessee at home, blowing a 24-point lead to lose by a score of 35–34.[292] Notre Dame would then lose back-to-back games for the first time since 1987 when they lost to unranked Penn State, their first loss to an unranked opponent also since 1987.[293] The Irish would close out the regular season with a victory over Hawaii by a score of 48–42.[294] The Irish would receive a berth in the Sugar Bowl in New Orleans, Louisiana, where they defeated Florida by a score of 39–28.[295]
