For all 2015 Texas A&M law graduates, 90.50% ultimately passed the bar exam within two years of graduation. That performance was also fifth-best among Texas law schools behind Baylor Law School (100.00%), SMU Dedman School of Law (94.12%), Texas Tech University School of Law (93.94%), and University of Houston Law Center (92.52%), while ahead of University of Texas School of Law (85.50%), Texas Southern University Thurgood Marshall School of Law (80.82%), South Texas College of Law (72.12%), and St. Mary's University School of Law (64.93%).[22]
