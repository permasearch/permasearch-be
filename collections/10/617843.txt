With the mystique of his graffiti name, his physical stature, and the reputation of his small parties, Herc became a folk hero in the Bronx. He began to play at nearby clubs including the Twilight Zone[7] Hevalo, Executive Playhouse, the PAL on 183rd Street,[5] as well as at high schools such as Dodge and Taft.[26] Rapping duties were delegated to Coke La Rock[27] and Theodore Puccio.[28] Herc's collective, known as The Herculoids, was augmented by Clark Kent and dancers The ##### Twins.[5] Herc took his soundsystem (the herculords) —still legendary for its sheer volume[29]—to the streets and parks of the Bronx.
