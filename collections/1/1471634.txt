The act defined an employee's "regular rate" of pay for purposes of computation of overtime pay.[24] The act specified that employees were covered by the Fair Labor Standards Act if they are "directly essential" to production of goods for interstate commerce.[24] The act increased the minimum wage from 40 cents to 75 cents per hour, effective January 24, 1950.[24] The act prohibited oppressive child labor in commerce or in the production of goods for commerce.[24] The act also included a few new exemptions for special worker classes.
