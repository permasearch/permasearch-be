Halo 3 is the final game in the main Halo trilogy, ending the story arc begun in Halo: Combat Evolved.[32] The game was released on the Xbox 360 on September 25, 2007.[33] It adds to the series vehicles, weapons, and a class of items called equipment.[34] The game also includes a limited map-editing tool, known as the Forge, which allows players to insert game objects, such as weapons and crates, into existing multiplayer map geometry.[35] Players can save a recording of their gameplay sessions, and view them as video, from any angle.[36]
