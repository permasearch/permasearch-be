Trump formally announced his candidacy on June 16, 2015, with a campaign rally and speech at Trump Tower in New York City. In his speech, Trump drew attention to domestic issues such as illegal immigration, offshoring of American jobs, the U.S. national debt, and Islamic terrorism. The campaign slogan was announced as "Make America Great Again".[77] Trump declared that he would self-fund his presidential campaign, and would refuse any money from donors and lobbyists.[78] Ladbrokes offered 150/1 odds of Trump winning the presidency.[79] The Hollywood Reporter reported the next day that a casting company had offered $50 to actors to attend the Trump event, "to wear t-shirts and carry signs and help cheer him in support of his announcement."[80]
