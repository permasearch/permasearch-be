Before the 2016 season began, the Yankees acquired closer Aroldis Chapman, who was traded in mid-season. While with the Yankees, the triumvirate of Betances, Chapman and Miller became known by fans as "No Runs–D.M.C., owing to the relievers' dominance of opposing hitters.[32] Betances was named to his third straight MLB All-Star Game. He became the Yankees closer after the Yankees traded Chapman and Miller.[32] For the third straight season, Betances struck out 100 batters. Overall in 2016, Betances made 73 appearances with 126 strikeouts, a 3−6 record, a 3.08 ERA, and 12 saves.
