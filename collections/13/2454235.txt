On May 10, 2012, two weeks after Columbia Pictures and Sony Pictures Animation announced production of The Smurfs 2, Variety reported that writers Karey Kirkpatrick and Chris Poche were developing a script for The Smurfs 3,[52] which was set for release on July 24, 2015, and later rescheduled for August 14, 2015.[53] In March 2014, Sony announced that it will reboot the series with a completely computer-animated film.[54][55] Directed by Kelly Asbury, the reboot, titled Smurfs: The Lost Village, was released on April 7, 2017.[56]
