Sophisticated counterfeits have been found in several Asian countries such as Cambodia,[170] China,[171] Indonesia, Laos, Thailand, and Vietnam, and are an important cause of avoidable death in those countries.[172] The WHO said that studies indicate that up to 40% of artesunate-based malaria medications are counterfeit, especially in the Greater Mekong region and have established a rapid alert system to enable information about counterfeit drugs to be rapidly reported to the relevant authorities in participating countries.[173] There is no reliable way for doctors or lay people to detect counterfeit drugs without help from a laboratory. Companies are attempting to combat the persistence of counterfeit drugs by using new technology to provide security from source to distribution.[174]
