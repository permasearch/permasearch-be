Since then, support for a united Ireland has further increased and for the first time saw a majority of respondents favouring this option: in December 2017, an opinion poll carried out by LucidTalk on more than 2,000 people saw 47.9% of respondents willing to vote for a united Ireland if a border poll was held in the event of a hard Brexit, against 45.4 for maintaining the status quo.[55] 6% of respondents were undecided, and less than 1% of respondents stated that they would not vote.
