In April 1982, Raymond sent out his 12th catalog; each catalog cost $3 (equivalent to $7.61 in 2017).[14] Catalog sales accounted for 55% of the company's $7 million annual sales in 1982.[14] The Victoria's Secret stores at this time were "a niche player" in the underwear market. The business was described as "more burlesque than Main Street."[16]
