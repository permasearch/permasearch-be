On March 17, 2012, Sony Pictures announced that it was pursuing a sequel to 21 Jump Street, signing a deal that would see Jonah Hill and Michael Bacall return to write a script treatment that would be again developed by Bacall.[18] The film was originally scheduled to be released on June 6, 2014.[19] On May 8, 2013, it was announced that the film would be pushed back a week until June 13, 2014.[20] In June 2013, it was announced the film would be titled 22 Jump Street.[21] In July 2013, Phil Lord and Christopher Miller confirmed they would return to direct the film.[22] On September 6, 2013, Amber Stevens joined the cast of the film.[12] On September 27, 2013, Kurt Russell mentioned that his son Wyatt turned down a role for The Hunger Games sequels to star in 22 Jump Street.[23] Principal photography began on September 28, 2013, in New Orleans, Louisiana, with shots in San Juan, Puerto Rico as well (acting for the shots in the movie as the spring break in "Puerto Mexico") and ended on December 15, 2013.[24][25]
