According to the U.S. Census Bureau, in 2011 49.1% of the U.S. population lived in a household where at least one household member received a government benefit including Social Security and Medicare.[19][not in citation given] This figure represented an increase from 30% in the early 1980s and 44.4% in 2008.[20] While federal outlay for welfare was reduced by half since 1996, programs such as food stamps have seen increases in spending.[21] In 2012, 35% of the U.S. population lived in a household that received government benefits, counting only means-tested programs such as food stamps, housing assistance and Medicaid.[22]
