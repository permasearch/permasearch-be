Health insurance is, in principle, mandatory for residents of Japan, but there is no penalty for the 10% of individuals who choose not to comply, making it optional in practice.[35][36] There are a total of eight health insurance systems in Japan,[37] with around 3,500 health insurers. According to Mark Britnell, it is widely recognised that there are too many small insurers.[38] They can be divided into two categories, Employees' Health Insurance (健康保険, Kenkō-Hoken) and National Health Insurance (国民健康保険, Kokumin-Kenkō-Hoken).
