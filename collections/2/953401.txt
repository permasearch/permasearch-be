Lowry was awarded an honorary Master of Arts degree by the University of Manchester in 1945, and Doctor of Letters in 1961. In April 1955 Lowry was elected as an Associate Member of the Royal Academy of Arts and in April 1962 became a full Royal Academician.[48] At the end of December of the same year his membership status evolved to that of Senior Academician having reached the age of 75.[48] He was given the freedom of the city of Salford in 1965.[21]
