By some accounts, Lord Fairfax and Leven also fled the battlefield with their routed troops, but this has recently been challenged, certainly in Leven's case. The most detailed account of Leven's flight was written by the biographer of Lieutenant Colonel James Somerville, who was present at the battle as a volunteer.[86] However, this second hand account was published only in 1679, and has been challenged by previously unused eye witness accounts.[87] These show most of the Covenanter infantry and cavalry units remained fighting until the end of the battle.
