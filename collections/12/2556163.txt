A cavity wall comprises two totally discrete walls, separated by an air gap, which serves both as barrier to moisture and heat.[23][24] Typically the main loads taken by the foundations are carried there by the inner leaf, and the major functions of the external leaf are to protect the whole from weather, and to provide a fitting aesthetic finish.
