Johnny Patterson's songs have been recorded by numerous artists over the years, including his great-grandson Duncan Patterson, and several plays have been produced about his life. He was a character in Stewart Parker's play Heavenly Bodies along with the figure of Dion Boucicault representing two different sides of the Irish theatre. The Barabbas theatre group produced Johnny Patterson: The Singing Irish Clown in 2010 and Johnny Patterson the Musical was written about his life and relationship with Bridget Donoghue by Declan Mangan and Mick Jones in 2009.[4] Johnny Patterson also had the honour of being painted by the famous Irish artist Jack Yeats in the 1928 painting The Singing Clown.
