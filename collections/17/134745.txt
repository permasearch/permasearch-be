In May 2013, The Hollywood Reporter reported that Marvel had a working script for Ms. Marvel.[467] In October 2014, Marvel announced the film would be titled Captain Marvel and feature Carol Danvers.[227] In April 2015, Nicole Perlman and Meg LeFauve were announced as screenwriters.[468] At the 2016 San Diego Comic-Con, Brie Larson was confirmed to play the role of Carol Danvers.[469] In April 2017, Anna Boden and Ryan Fleck were hired to direct.[455] That August, Geneva Robertson-Dworet was revealed to be taking over as the film's screenwriter, replacing Perlman and LeFauve.[470] Liz Flahive, Carly Mensch, Boden and Ryan Fleck are also credited as screenwriters on the film.[456] Location filming occurred in January 2018,[471][472] while principal photography began in March 2018 in Los Angeles,[473] and is scheduled to last until May 2018.[474] The film is scheduled to be released on March 8, 2019.[209]
