After turning down several supporting roles, Deschanel made her leading debut in All the Real Girls (2003) as Noel, a sexually curious 18-year-old virgin who has a life-changing romance with an aimless 22-year-old. The film screened at the Sundance Film Festival and was released for selected theaters. Her performance received critical praise,[11] and she was nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Actress.[19] Later in 2003, Deschanel co-starred opposite Will Ferrell in the Christmas comedy Elf as a deadpan department store worker.[20] Response towards the film was positive and it grossed US$220.4 million at the worldwide box office.[21]
