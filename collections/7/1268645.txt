These geopolitical calculations were debated and discussed in the following years.[145] Historians agree that the British believed that expressing support would appeal to Jews in Germany and the United States, given two of Woodrow Wilson's closest advisors were known to be avid Zionists;[xxix][xxx][309] they also hoped to encourage support from the large Jewish population in Russia.[310] In addition, the British intended to pre-empt the expected French pressure for an international administration in Palestine.[xxxi]
