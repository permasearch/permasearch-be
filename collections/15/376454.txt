In the American colonial period before 1776, and for some time after, often only adult white male property owners could vote; enslaved Africans, most free black people and most women were not extended the franchise.[58] On the American frontier, democracy became a way of life, with more widespread social, economic and political equality.[59] Although not described as a democracy by the founding fathers,[60] they shared a determination to root the American experiment in the principles of natural freedom and equality.[61]
