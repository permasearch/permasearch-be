In his teen years "Sonny", as he was known to his friends, aimed to become a baseball player, and was also nicknamed "The Actor".[6] Pacino went to the Herman Ridder Junior High School,[7] but in secondary school dropped out of many classes except English. He subsequently attended the High School of Performing Arts,[8] but dropped out of school at age 17. His mother disagreed with his decision and after an argument, he left home. To finance his acting studies, he took low-paying jobs as messenger, busboy, janitor, and postal clerk[3], and once worked in the mail room for Commentary magazine.[9]
