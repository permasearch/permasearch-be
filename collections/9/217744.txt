The Hasmoneans continually extended their control over much of the region.[37] In 125 BCE the Hasmonean ethnarch John Hyrcanus subjugated Edom and forcibly converted the population to Judaism.[38] Hyrcanus' son Alexander Jannaeus established good relations with the Roman Republic, however there was growing tension between Pharisees and Sadducces and a conflict over the succession to Janneus, in which the warring parties invited foreign intervention on their behalf.
