After having entered the 2016 season as an underdog, Thielen ended up leading the Vikings with 967 receiving yards, while also adding a career-high 69 catches and five touchdowns.[27][28] His 10.0 yards per target and 75.0% catch rate ranked in the top five players among NFL wide receivers.[29] Thielen dropped only two passes on 92 targets according to Sporting Charts, and Pro Football Focus ranked him as the 18th best receiver in the league.[30] For his efforts, NFL.com named Thielen as their "Unsung Hero" for the Minnesota Vikings.[31]
