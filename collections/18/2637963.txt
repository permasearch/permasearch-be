The Japanese had their own motive for deliberately spreading the usage of the term Manchuria.[14] The historian Norman Smith wrote that "The term "Manchuria" is controversial".[15] Professor Mariko Asano Tamanoi said that she "should use the term in quotation marks", when referring to Manchuria.[16] Herbert Giles wrote that "Manchuria" was unknown to the Manchus themselves as a geographical expression.[17] In his doctoral thesis of 2012, Professor Chad D. Garcia noted that usage of the term "Manchuria" was out of favor in "current scholarly practice" and preferred the term "the northeast".[18]
