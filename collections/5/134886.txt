By 1781, the Army numbered approximately 121,000 men globally,[21] 48,000 of whom were stationed throughout the Americas.[20] Of the 171,000 sailors[25] who served in the Royal Navy throughout the conflict, around a quarter were pressed. This same proportion, approximately 42,000 men, deserted during the conflict.[38] At its height, the Navy had 94 ships-of-the-line,[482] 104 frigates[483] and 37 sloops[484] in service.
