Gerrymandering (Irish: Claonroinnt) is widely considered to have been introduced after the establishment of Home Rule in Northern Ireland in 1921, favouring Unionists who tended to be Protestant, to the detriment of Nationalists who were mostly Catholic.[citation needed] Some critics and supporters spoke at the time of "A Protestant Parliament for a Protestant People".
