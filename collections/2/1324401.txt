British Borneo comprised the four northern parts of the island of Borneo, which are now Brunei, Labuan, Sabah, and Sarawak.[1][2] During the British colonial rule until 1963, Sarawak, Labuan and Sabah known as: Kingdom of Sarawak (1841–1946) – Crown Colony of Sarawak (1946–1963) (now Sarawak), Crown Colony of Labuan (1848–1946) (now Labuan) and North Borneo (1881–1946) – Crown Colony of North Borneo (1946–1963) (now Sabah).[3]
