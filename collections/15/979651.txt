Chilean  mitochondrial DNA and Y-chromosome studies indicate that European ancestry predominates in the Chilean upper class;[65] in the middle class the European component ranges from 72.3 to 76.8 percent,[65][66] and the Amerindian population ranges from 23.2 to 27.7 percent.[65][66] In the lower socioeconomic class, European ancestry is 62.9%–65 percent[65][66] and Amerindian ancestry is 35–37.1 percent.[65][66]
