A free agent when the NHL resumed play in 2005–06, Selänne returned to Anaheim by signing a one-year, $1 million contract with the Mighty Ducks.[38] During a resurgent season, he scored his 1,000th career point on January 30, 2006, with a goal in a 4–3 victory over Los Angeles.[39] Selänne completed the season with 40 goals and 90 points, both of which led the Mighty Ducks,[5] and he was named the recipient of the Bill Masterton Memorial Trophy for dedication and perseverance.[40]
