Texas was admitted to the United States on December 29, 1845 and elects its U.S. Senators to Class 1 and Class 2. The state's current officeholders are Republicans Senate Majority Whip John Cornyn (serving since 2002) and Ted Cruz (serving since 2013). A total of 27 Democrats, 7 Republicans, and 1 Liberal Republican have served or are serving as U.S. Senators from Texas.
