Shortly after the Lady Windermere syndrome was proposed, a librarian wrote a letter to the editor of Chest[23] challenging the use of Lady Windermere as the eponymous ancestor of the proposed syndrome. In the play, Lady Windermere is a vivacious young woman, married only two years, who never coughs or displays any other signs of illness.
