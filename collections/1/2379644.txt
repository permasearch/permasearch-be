Paul V. Kroskrity (/ˈkrɑːskrɪti/; born February 10, 1949) is an American linguistic anthropologist known primarily for his contributions to establishing and developing language ideology as a field of research.[1] He is professor of anthropology, applied linguistics, and American Indian Studies at the University of California, Los Angeles. He is currently the President of the Society for Linguistic Anthropology[2] and the Chair of the American Indian Studies program at the University of California, Los Angeles.[3]
