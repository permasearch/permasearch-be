In 1952, Sight and Sound magazine revealed the results of its first poll for "The Best Films of All Time"; City Lights was voted #2, after Vittorio DeSica's Bicycle Thieves.[64] In 2002, City Lights ranked 45th on the critics' list.[65] That same year, directors were polled separately and ranked the film as 19th overall.[66] In 1992, the Library of Congress selected City Lights for preservation in the United States National Film Registry as being "culturally, historically, or aesthetically significant."[67] In 2007, the American Film Institute's tenth anniversary edition of 100 Years... 100 Movies ranked City Lights as the 11th greatest American film of all time, an improvement over the 76th position on the original list.[68] AFI also chose the film as the best romantic comedy of American cinema in 2008's "10 Top 10".[69] The Tramp was number 38 on AFI's list of the 50 Best Heroes,[70] and the film ranked at 38th among the funniest films,[71] 10th among the greatest love stories,[72] and 33rd on the most inspiring films.[73]
