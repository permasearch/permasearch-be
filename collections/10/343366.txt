The Barelvi movement is a Sufi-influenced revivalist movement within Sunni Islam with over 200 million followers,[306] largely in South Asia.[307][308] Sufism enjoyed a strong revival in central Asia and South Asia. Central Asia is considered to be a center of Sufism. Sufism has played a significant role in fighting against Tsars of Russia and Soviet colonization.[citation needed] Here, Sufis and their different orders are the main religious sources.[309][310] Sufism is also strong in African countries such as Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco, Senegal, Chad and Niger.[311][312]
