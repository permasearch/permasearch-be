Japan's spendings on roads has been considered large.[98] The 1.2 million kilometers of paved road are one of the major means of transportation.[99] Japan has left-hand traffic.[100] A single network of speed, divided, limited-access toll roads connects major cities and are operated by toll-collecting enterprises.[101] New and used cars are inexpensive, and the Japanese government has encouraged people to buy hybrid vehicles.[102] Car ownership fees and fuel levies are used to promote energy-efficiency.[102]
