Carthage produced finely embroidered silks,[123] dyed textiles of cotton, linen,[124] and wool, artistic and functional pottery, faience, incense, and perfumes.[125] Its artisans worked expertly with ivory,[126] glassware, and wood,[127] as well as with alabaster, bronze, brass, lead, gold, silver, and precious stones to create a wide array of goods, including mirrors, furniture[128] and cabinetry, beds, bedding, and pillows,[129] jewelry, arms, implements, and household items.[130] It traded in salted Atlantic fish and fish sauce (garum),[131] and brokered the manufactured, agricultural, and natural products[132] of almost every Mediterranean people.[133]
