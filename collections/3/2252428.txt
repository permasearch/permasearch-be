The encomiums were not confined to his batting gifts; nor did the criticism extend to his character. "Australia has unearthed a champion," said former Australian Test great Clem Hill, "self-taught, with natural ability. But most important of all, with his heart in the right place."[33] Selector Dick Jones weighed in with the observation that it was "good to watch him talking to an old player, listening attentively to everything that is said and then replying with a modest 'thank you'."[33]
