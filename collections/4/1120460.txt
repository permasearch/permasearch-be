As of the census[2] of 2010, there were 31,145 people, 11,230 households, and 8,286 families residing in the CDP.[7] The population density was 1,126.5 people per square mile (434.9/km²). There were 8,257 housing units at an average density of 443.6/sq mi (171.2/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 86.9% White, 6.2% African American, 0.1% Native American, 3.4% Asian, 2.0% from other races, and 1.4% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 11.7% of the population.[8] The subdivision of Carpenter's Run was used for filming the 1990 film Edward Scissorhands.[9]
