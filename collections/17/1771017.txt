Discussions on anti-Americanism have in most cases lacked a precise explanation of what the sentiment entails (other than a general disfavor), which has led to the term being used broadly and in an impressionistic manner, resulting in the inexact impressions of the many expressions described as anti-American.[4] William Russell Melton argues that criticism largely originates from the perception that the U.S. wants to act as a "world policeman".[5]
