With Iger in place as the new CEO of Disney, Steve Jobs resumed negotiations for Pixar with Disney.[190] On January 24, 2006, Disney announced that it would acquire Pixar for $7.4 billion, with the deal closing that May.[190][191] and the Circle 7 studio launched to produce Toy Story 3 was shut down,[192][193] with most of its employees returning to Feature Animation and Toy Story 3 returning to Pixar's control.[194] As part of the acquisition, Edwin Catmull and John Lasseter were named President and Chief Creative Officer, respectively, of Feature Animation as well as Pixar.
