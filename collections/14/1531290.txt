In March 2000, Bill Clinton, the President of the United States, paid a state visit to India.[68] This was the first state visit to India by a U.S. President in 22 years since President Jimmy Carter's visit in 1978.[69] President Clinton's visit was hailed as a significant milestone in relations between the two nations.[68] Vajpayee and Clinton had wide-ranging discussions on bilateral, regional and international developments.[70] The visit led to expansion in trade and economic ties between India and the United States.[71] A vision document on the future course of Indo-U.S. relations was signed during the visit.[72]
