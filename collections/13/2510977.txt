Fibonacci (c. 1175 – c. 1250)[2] was an Italian mathematician from the Republic of Pisa, considered to be "the most talented Western mathematician of the Middle Ages".[3] The name he is commonly called, "Fibonacci" (Italian: [fiboˈnattʃi][4]), was made up in 1838 by the French historian Guillaume Libri[5] and is short for "filius Bonacci" ("son of (the) Bonacci")[6] and he is also known as Leonardo Bonacci, Leonardo of Pisa, Leonardo Pisano Bigollo, or Leonardo Fibonacci.
