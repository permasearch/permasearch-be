Wolves' group parent company, which wholly owns both the "football club company" and the company holding its properties (including its stadium and training facilities), has net assets valued at almost £50 million.[110] In their last published accounts (covering the 2015–16 season), the club achieved a final pre-tax profit of £5.8 million.[111] Turnover rose to £27.25 million, with the club receiving its final year of "parachute payments" from the Premier League.[112] Like most football clubs, significant commercial income is generated from shirt sponsorship deals:[113] Its current affiliation, with the payday lender The Money Shop, is due to run until at least the end of the 2018–19 season.[114]
