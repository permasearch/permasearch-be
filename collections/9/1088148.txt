Philadelphia in the southeast corner, Pittsburgh in the southwest corner, Erie in the northwest corner, Scranton-Wilkes-Barre in the northeast corner, and Allentown-Bethlehem-Easton in the east central region are urban manufacturing centers. Much of the Commonwealth is rural; this dichotomy affects state politics as well as the state economy.[105] Philadelphia is home to six Fortune 500 companies,[106] with more located in suburbs like King of Prussia; it is a leader in the financial[107] and insurance industry.
