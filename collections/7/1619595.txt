On 16 August, police opened fire on a group of miners who had gathered on a hill near Nkaneng,at least 34 people were killed at Marikana, 78 were injured and 259 were arrested.[15] The miners were carrying machetes and had refused a request to disarm. According to the Congress of South African Trade Unions, police had first used tear gas, water cannons and then used "live ammunition".[16] The killings have been labelled a massacre throughout the media with police, Lonmin and NUM itself being blamed.[17][18][19]
