Every respectable man generally wore the simlāh over the kethōneth (See Isaiah 20:2-3), but since the simlāh hindered work, it was either left home or removed when working.[1][5] (See Matthew 24:18). From this simple item of the common people developed the richly ornamented mantle of the well-off, which reached from the neck to the knees and had short sleeves.[5]
