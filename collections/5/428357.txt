Average hours of sunshine in winter range from 38–108 hours in some mountainous areas and western Scotland, up to 217 hours in the south and east of England;[38] while average hours of sunshine in summer range from 294–420 hours in northern Scotland and Northern Ireland, to 600–760 hours in southern English coastal counties.[39] The most sunshine recorded in one month was 383.9 hours at Eastbourne (East Sussex) in July 1911.[37]
