"See the Day" became Girls Aloud's lowest charting single to date when it entered the UK Singles Chart at number nine, behind other new entries by Shayne Ward and Eminem at numbers one and five respectively.[16][17] The single fell just one position the following week, rounding out the top ten.[18] It swiftly fell down the charts, falling to number nineteen in its third week.[19] The song spent a total of just six weeks in the UK's top 75.[16][20] "See the Day" made Girls Aloud the first girl group to achieve eleven consecutive top ten singles. The song was also an airplay hit.[3] It was, however, less successful on the official UK Download Chart, where "See the Day" debuted at number 29.[21]
