Iran's avant-garde modernists emerged by the arrival of new western influences during World War II.[400] The vibrant contemporary art scene originates in the late 1940s, and Tehran's first modern art gallery, Apadana, was opened in September 1949 by painters Mahmud Javadipur, Hosein Kazemi, and Hushang Ajudani.[401][402] The new movements received official encouragement by mid-1950s,[400] which led to the emergence of artists such as Marcos Grigorian, signaling a commitment to the creation of a form of modern art grounded in Iran.[403]
