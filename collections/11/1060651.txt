Few Indian commercial crops—such as Cotton, indica, opium, wheat, and rice—made it to the global market under the British Raj in India.[66] The second half of the 19th century saw some increase in land under cultivation and agricultural production expanded at an average rate of about 1% per year by the later 19th century.[67] Due to extensive irrigation by canal networks Punjab, Narmada valley, and Andhra Pradesh became centres of agrarian reforms.
