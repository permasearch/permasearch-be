The United States is home to the second largest passenger vehicle market of any country in the world second now[when?] to China.[1] Overall, there were an estimated 263.6 million registered passenger vehicles in the United States in 2015.[2] This number, along with the average age of vehicles, has increased steadily since 1960. The United States is also home to three large vehicle manufacturers: General Motors, Ford Motor Company, and Chrysler, which have historically been referred to as the "Big Three".
