He was born at Glasgow the son of William Muir, and younger brother of John Muir. He was educated at Kilmarnock Academy, the universities of Glasgow and Edinburgh, and at Haileybury College.[1] In 1837 he entered the Bengal civil service. Muir served as secretary to the governor of the North-West Provinces, and as a member of the Agra revenue board, and during the Mutiny he was in charge of the intelligence department there. In 1865 he was made foreign secretary to the Indian Government. In 1867 Muir was knighted (K.C.S.I.), and in 1868 he became lieutenant-governor of the North Western Provinces.[2]
