Pocahontas has been considered in popular culture to be a princess. In 1841, William Watson Waldron of Trinity College, Dublin, in Ireland, published Pocahontas, American Princess: and Other Poems, calling Pocahontas "the beloved and only surviving daughter of the king".[24] Indeed, Pocahontas was a favourite of her father's—his "delight and darling", according to the colonist Captain Ralph Hamor[25]—but she was not in line to inherit a position as a weroance, subchief, or mamanatowick (paramount chief).
