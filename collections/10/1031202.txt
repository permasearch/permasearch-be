Turkish tea is an important part of that country's cuisine, and is the most commonly consumed hot drink, despite the country's long history of coffee consumption. In 2004 Turkey produced 205,500 tonnes of tea (6.4% of the world's total tea production), which made it one of the largest tea markets in the world,[88] with 120,000 tons being consumed in Turkey, and the rest being exported.[89] In 2010 Turkey had the highest per capita consumption in the world at 2.7 kg.[90] As of 2013, the per-capita consumption of Turkish tea exceeds 10 cups per day and 13.8 kg per year.[91] Tea is grown mostly in Rize Province on the Black Sea coast.[92]
