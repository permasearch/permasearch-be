James Abram Garfield (November 19, 1831 – September 19, 1881) was the 20th President of the United States. An assassin's bullet ended his life and presidency and cut his time in office after serving only 200 days.[53][54] He had a distinguished military background. Garfield served in the United States Army as a Major General, as a member of the U.S. House of Representatives,[55] and as a member of the highly controversial Electoral Commission of 1876. He was the second U.S. President to be assassinated.[56] Garfield was the only sitting member of the House of Representatives to have been elected president.[57]
