Al Smith and later John F. Kennedy were the political heroes for Catholics.[185] Al Smith, who had an Irish mother and an English-German father, in 1928 became the first Catholic to run for president.[186] From the 1830s to the 1960s, Irish Catholics voted heavily Democratic, with occasional exceptions like the election of 1920. Their precincts showed average support levels of 80%.[187]  As historian Lawrence McCaffrey  notes, "until recently they have been so closely associated with the Democratic party that Irish, Catholic, and Democrat composed a trinity of associations, serving mutual interests and needs. "[188]
