The Internet in South Africa, one of the most technologically resourced countries on the African continent, is expanding. The Internet country code top-level domain (CcLD)[1] .za is managed and regulated by the .ZA Domain Name Authority and was granted to South Africa by ICANN in 1990. Over 60% of Internet traffic generated on the African continent originates from South Africa. As of July 2016 29,322,380 people (54.0% of the total population) were Internet users.[2]
