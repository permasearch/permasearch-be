Induráin now lives near Pamplona and has a house in Benidorm, on the Mediterranean.[26] He and his wife, Marisa, have three children.[4] He founded the Miguel Induráin Foundation in 1998 to promote sport in his home region of Navarra. He worked with the Spanish Olympic Committee to promote Sevilla's candidature for the 2004 Olympics,[1] and the Union Cycliste Internationale. He continues to ride a bike three or four times a week.[4] He attends cyclotourist events such as L'Étape du Tour,[27] the Mallorca312 and the Cape Argus Pick & Pay Cycle Tour in Cape Town, South Africa. On the 2nd of September 2017 he raced redhook crit in Barcelona.
