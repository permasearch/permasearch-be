In the week ending on September 23, 2009, the album debuted at number four on the US Billboard 200, selling 104,000 copies in its first week of release, charting behind Jay-Z's The Blueprint 3, Whitney Houston's I Look to You and Muse's The Resistance.[45] On the 2009 year-end chart for Billboard 200, Man on the Moon: The End of Day was listed at number 157.[46] The album also charted and peaked at the same position on the US Rap Albums chart.[47] It failed to rise above its positions on those charts and would later be outperformed by his next studio album, Man on the Moon II: The Legend of Mr. Rager (2010).[48] Man on the Moon: The End of Day also peaked on the US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums top ten positions.[49]
