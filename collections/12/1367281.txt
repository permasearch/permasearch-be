The wars in the novels are much more morally complex than a fight between good and evil.[41] The novels are to reflect that wars have substantial death rates.[19] The novels' attitude toward war is shaped by Martin's experiences with the controversies of the Vietnam War.[41] As Martin was against the Vietnam War, the books reflect some of his views on war and violence and their costs, though he endeavors to avoid using his characters as mouthpieces for his own personal diatribes.[11]
