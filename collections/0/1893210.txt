Spokane is close to dozens of lakes and rivers for outdoor sports and recreation. People use these for swimming, boating, rafting, and fishing. Nearby mountains provide for skiing, hiking, biking and sightseeing.[245] Spokane's professional and semi-professional sports teams include the Spokane Indians in Minor League Baseball and Spokane Chiefs in junior ice hockey.[246] Collegiate sports in Spokane focus on the local teams such as the Gonzaga Bulldogs that compete in the NCAA's Division I West Coast Conference and the Whitworth Pirates playing in the Division III Northwest Conference as well as other regional teams, including the Washington State Cougars, Eastern Washington Eagles, and the Idaho Vandals.[246]
