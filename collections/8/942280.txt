After the copyrights on the operas expired, numerous companies around the world released popular audio and video recordings of the operas.[111][124] In 1966 and again in the 1980s, BBC Radio presented complete cycles of the thirteen extant Gilbert and Sullivan operas, with dialogue.[125] Ad hoc casts of operatic singers conducted by Sir Malcolm Sargent in the 1950s and 60s[126] and Sir Charles Mackerras in the 1990s[111] have made audio sets of several Savoy operas, and in the 1980s Alexander Faris conducted video recordings of eleven of the operas (omitting the last two) with casts including show-business stars as well as professional singers.[127] Joseph Papp's Broadway production of The Pirates of Penzance was put on record in 1981.[128][129] Since 1994, the International Gilbert and Sullivan Festival has released numerous professional and amateur CDs and videos of its productions.[130] Ohio Light Opera has recorded several of the operas in the 21st century.[131]
