Midway through her second term, Merkel's approval plummeted in Germany, resulting in heavy losses in state elections for her party.[90] An August 2011 poll found her coalition had only 36% support compared to a rival potential coalition's 51%.[91] However, she scored well on her handling of the recent euro crisis (69% rated her performance as good rather than poor), and her approval rating reached an all-time high of 77% in February 2012 and again in July 2014.[92] Merkel's approval rating dropped to 54% in October 2015, during the European migrant crisis, the lowest since 2011.[93] According to a poll conducted after terror attacks in Germany Merkel's approval rating dropped to 47% (August 2016).[94] Half of Germans did not want her to serve a fourth term in office compared to 42% in favor.[95] However, according to a poll taken in October 2016, her approval rating had been found to have risen again, 54% of Germans were found to be satisfied with work of Merkel as Chancellor.[96] According to another poll taken in November 2016, 59% were to found to be in favour of a renewed Chancellor candidature of Merkel in 2017.[97] According to a poll carried out just days after the 2016 Berlin attack, in which it was asked which political leader(s) Germans trust to solve their country's problems; 56% named Merkel, 39% Seehofer (CSU), 35% Gabriel (SPD), 32% Schulz (SPD), 25% Ã–zdemir (Greens), 20% Wagenknecht (Left party), 15% Lindner (FDP), and just 10% for Petry (AfD).[98] A YouGov survey published in late December 2017 found that just 36 percent of all respondents want Merkel to stay at the helm until 2021, while half of those surveyed voters called for a change at the top before the end of the legislature.[99]
