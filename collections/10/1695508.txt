The Supplementary Benefits Act of 1976 introduced new levels of financial support for those on little or no income,[120] while new benefits were introduced for those with disabilities.[121] For families with children, the system of child cash and tax allowances was replaced in 1977 by a universal Child Benefit for all families with at least one child,[122] For those at work, a supplementary pension scheme was introduced to provide members of the workforce with an additional income in retirement.[123]
