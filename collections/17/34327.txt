SR was the smallest of four groups that were brought together by the Railways Act 1921.[167] The LB&SCR had partly electrified their network before World War I, however that had been an overhead system, SR decided to electrify their network using the third rail DC system.[167] During World War II the SR was heavily involved with transporting armed services traffic and was bombed on many occasions.[167] After the war SR was nationalised in 1948, and became the Southern Region of British Railways.[167]
