The NRA's Political Victory Fund super PAC spent $11.2 million in the 2012 election cycle,[75] and as of April 2014, it had raised $13.7 million for 2014 elections.[76] Michael Bloomberg's gun-control super PAC, Independence USA, spent $8.3 million in 2012[77][78] and $6.3 million in 2013.[79] Americans for Responsible Solutions, another gun-control super PAC started by retired Congresswoman Gabrielle Giffords, raised $12 million in 2013,[80] and plans to raise $16 to $20 million by the 2014 elections.[81] The group's treasurer said that the funds would be enough to compete with the NRA "on an even-keel basis."[81]
