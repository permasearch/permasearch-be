Tybee Island is located at 32°0′24″N 80°50′58″W﻿ / ﻿32.00667°N 80.84944°W﻿ / 32.00667; -80.84944 (32.006672, -80.849374).[15] The island is the northeastern-most of Georgia's Sea Islands, which comprise the outer section of the state's Lower Coastal Plain region. Like the other Sea Islands, Tybee consists of a sandy beach on its eastern shore and a tidal salt marsh on its western shore. The interior consists of a maritime forest (the density of which has been reduced by development) and freshwater sloughs.[16]
