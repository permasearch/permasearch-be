The school is ranked first among public universities in enrollment of National Merit Scholars and among the top ten in the graduation of Rhodes Scholars.[7] PC Magazine and the Princeton Review rated it one of the "20 Most Wired Colleges" in both 2006[9] and 2008,[10] while the Carnegie Foundation classifies it as a research university with "very high research activity."[11] Its Norman campus has two prominent museums, the Fred Jones Jr. Museum of Art, specializing in French Impressionism and Native American artwork, and the Sam Noble Oklahoma Museum of Natural History, specializing in the natural history of Oklahoma.
