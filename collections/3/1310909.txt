Villains which debuted during this early era included the Mad Hatter in issue #49 (October 1948)[9] and Killer Moth in issue #63 (February 1951).[10]  In 1953, Sheldon Moldoff became another one of the primary Batman ghost artists who, along with Win Mortimer and Dick Sprang, drew stories credited to Bob Kane, following Kane's style and under Kane's supervision.[11] Bill Finger and Moldoff introduced Ace the Bat-Hound in #92 (June 1955).[12]
