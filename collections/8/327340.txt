During a midnight congressional session starting January 11, 2017, the Senate of the 115th Congress of the United States voted to approve a "budget blueprint" which would allow Republicans to repeal parts of the law "without threat of a Democratic filibuster."[394][395] The plan, which passed 51-48, is a budget blueprint named by Senate Republicans the "Obamacare 'repeal resolution.'"[396] Democrats opposing the resolution staged a protest during the vote.[397]
