North Indian temples are referred to as Nagara style of temple architecture.[128] They have sanctum sanctorum where the deity is present, open on one side from where the devotee obtains dar≈õana. There may or may not be many more surrounding corridors, halls, etc. However, there will be space for devotees to go around the temple in clockwise fashion circumambulation. In North Indian temples, the tallest towers are built over the sanctum sanctorum in which the deity is installed.[129]
