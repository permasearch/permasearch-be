Dishwashing liquid has uses as an ingredient in making homemade garden pest deterrents. Oregon State University's Cooperative Extension Service notes the use of dishwashing liquid to get rid of spidermites.[30] Dish soap has also been used to deter aphids.[30][31] In some instances, the dish soap may be toxic to plant leaves and cause them to "burn".[30] Use of soap or dish detergent to help spread pesticide on plants is noted by University of Georgia extension service, but not recommended.[32]
