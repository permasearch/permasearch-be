Kingston-upon-Thames Council and the Electoral Commission stated that Jakub Pawlowski, a Polish voter in Kingston-upon-Thames declared himself as being British on his registration form, and hence, received a referendum polling card in the post, although he is not a UK citizen and did not have the right to receive such a polling card. The voter stated that he specified that he is a Polish citizen when registering on the electoral roll,[56] but, still received the card in the post.[57] The matter had been referred to the police.[58]
