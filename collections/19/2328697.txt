By 1966, he began to expand outside the capital, starting with Lille.[30] Within a short time, Borel had 20 locations; 15 in Paris and five in the rest of the country.[28] Although successful, Borel was forced to close the chain in 1969, due to a disagreement he had with Lyons, the British parent company.[31]
