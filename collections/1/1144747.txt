Land Rover extended the 3,946 cc engine for the top LSE[7] specification of the Classic Range Rover. The 4.2 L engine had a displacement of 4,275 cc (260.9 cu in), and used the crankshaft castings from the failed "Iceberg" diesel engine project.[8] Bore remained the same at 94.0 mm (3.70 in), while stroke increased to 77.0 mm (3.03 in).
