The song received further controversy in September 2017 when the music video was released depicting a white child being hung on stage in addition to other various depictions of murder and death.[21][22][23] Although heavily criticized by conservative news outlets,[24][25] Onfroy clarified via Instagram that the video was meant to be taken seriously as a form of art, and that it was a form of shock value.[26] Soon after, Onfroy claimed on an Instagram video that a member of the Ku Klux Klan had threatened him for the depictions in the song.[27][28]
