He began the 2012 season with a world-leading 100 m time of 9.82 seconds in May.[169] He took victories over Asafa Powell with runs of 9.76 seconds in Rome and 9.79 in Oslo.[170] At the Jamaican Athletics Championships he was outdone by Yohan Blake, first in the 200 m and then in the 100 m, with his younger rival setting world leading times.[171][172]
