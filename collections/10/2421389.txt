Brooks' second album, No Fences, was released in 1990 and spent 23 weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard Top Country Albums chart.[25] The album also reached No. 3 on the |Billboard 200, and eventually became Brooks' highest-selling album, with domestic shipments of 17 million.[26] It contained what would become Brooks' signature song, the blue collar anthem "Friends in Low Places", as well as other popular singles, "The Thunder Rolls" and "Unanswered Prayers".
