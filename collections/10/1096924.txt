The deal was a landmark in the housing crisis, given Countrywide's prominence as the nation's largest mortgage lender, at the time.[54] It was to prove more costly to Bank of America, however, than was perceived at the time: in August 2014, B of A was forced to agree to a near-$17 billion deal to settle claims against it relating to the sale of toxic mortgage-linked securities, a large percentage of which had been sold by Countrywide.[55]
