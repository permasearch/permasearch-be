In 1981, Edward T. Foote II became the school's fourth president.[47] Under Foote's leadership, on campus student housing was converted into a system of residential colleges.[48] In addition, Foote initiated a five-year $400 million fundraising campaign that began in 1984 and raised $517.5 million.[49][50] He saw the endowment expand from $47.4 million in 1981 to $465.2 million in 2000.[49]
