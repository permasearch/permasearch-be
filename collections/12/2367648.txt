The Indian railways are subsidized by around ₹400 billion (US$6.1 billion), of which around 60% goes to commuter rail and short-haul trips.[79][80] It is the fourth largest railway network in the world comprising 119,630 kilometres (74,330 mi) of total track and 92,081 km (57,216 mi) of running track over a route of 66,687 km (41,437 mi) with 7,216 stations at the end of 2015–16.
