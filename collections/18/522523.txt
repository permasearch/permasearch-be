The film won the Academy Award for Best Sound Editing; it also received nominations for Best Original Score and Best Sound (Ben Burtt, Gary Summers, Shawn Murphy and Tony Dawe), but lost to The Little Mermaid and Glory respectively.[49] Sean Connery received a Golden Globe Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor.[50] Connery and the visual and sound effects teams were also nominated at the 43rd British Academy Film Awards.[51] The film won the 1990 Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation,[52] and was nominated for Best Motion Picture Drama at the Young Artist Awards.[53] John Williams' score won a BMI Award, and was nominated for a Grammy Award.[54]
