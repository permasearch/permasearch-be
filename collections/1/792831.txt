A 2.5 metre (8 ft) bronze version of the sculpture stands in Leonardo’s birthplace, Vinci, Italy, where it was dedicated on November 17, 2001.[9] Made possible with gifts from several benefactors, including Peter F. Secchia, the former United States Ambassador to Italy, and his wife, Joan, The Vinci horse inspired a sister city relationship between Vinci, Italy, and Allentown, Pa. A plaza in Vinci, Italy, also was named in the memory of Charles C. Dent.
