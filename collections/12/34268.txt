After the Reform Act of 1832 Sussex was divided into the eastern division and the western division and two representatives were elected for each division.[6] In June 1832 the Honorable C.C. Cavendish and H.B. Curteis Esquire were elected in the eastern division and the Earl of Surrey and Lord John George Lennox were elected for the western division.[6] There was a total of 3478 votes cast in the eastern division and 2365 votes in the western division.[6]
