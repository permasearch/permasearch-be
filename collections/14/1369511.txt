Brazilian club Grêmio qualified for their first Club World Cup by winning the 2017 Copa Libertadores in November. They entered the semi-finals round, facing North American champions C.F. Pachuca of Mexico, who had defeated African champions Wydad Casablanca of Morocco in the quarter-finals.[9] Grêmio won the match 1–0 in extra time, on a goal scored by Éverton in the 95th minute.[10]
