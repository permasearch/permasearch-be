Economic recovery from the historic downturn started in the late 2009 with exports rising 22.8 percent.[21] The economic prospects for 2010 in the early 2009 were of a positive growth of 3.5 [22] and some saw a steady recovery by the second quarter of 2010.[23] At the end of 2010, the OECD revealed an estimated growth of 4.5 percent[24] while the Mexican government estimated a growth of over 5 percent[25] and the creation of 730 thousand jobs.[26]
