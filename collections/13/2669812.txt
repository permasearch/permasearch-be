Ash fall extended as far as the capital, Guatemala City forcing the closure of La Aurora International Airport.  The military assisted in clearing ash off of the runway. Rescue attempts were seriously hampered as routes into the affected regions were seriously damaged by the pyroclastic density currents.[28]. On 5 June[29], Associated Press reported that at least 99 people are dead and nearly 200 others unaccounted for following the eruption[26][29], while some Shriners hospitals were activated for new entries[26].
