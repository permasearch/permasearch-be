In his first season of 1900, he coached the Tigers to their first undefeated season (6–0),[3] and first conference championship, outscoring their opponents 222–10 – the 64–0 win over Davidson on opening day was then the largest score ever made in the South.[9] The season had various other "firsts", including the school's first defeat of the Georgia Bulldogs and the Alabama Crimson Tide.
