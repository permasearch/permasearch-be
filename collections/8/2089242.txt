The company was established by John W. Tyson in 1935,[3] and benefitted during World War II when chicken was not included in foods that were rationed.[4] As of 2014, the company employs 115,000 people, who work at more than 300 facilities, over 100 of which are in the US.[3] Tyson had about 97,000 employees in 27 states; locations are concentrated in the Midwest, with 16 locations in Arkansas, 11 in Texas, 9 in Iowa, and the remainder of the mostly eastern US with less than 3-4 locations.[3] Tyson also works with 6,729 independent contract chicken growers.[citation needed]
