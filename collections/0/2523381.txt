On May 16, a low-pressure area moved into the Gulf of Aden and developed into a depression, receiving the designation ARB 02. [7] On the next day, the system strengthened into a cyclonic storm and was given the name Sagar.[8] On May 18, the system strengthened and organized into a small and compact cyclone, with a rough eye feature.[9] Under the influence of a ridge anchored over the Arabian Sea, the cyclone moved southwestward, and made landfall on Somalia between 1:30 PM and 2:30 PM (IST) on May 19.[10]
