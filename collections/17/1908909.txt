On July 31, 2008, Bush announced that with the end of July, American troop deaths had reached their lowest number—thirteen—since the war began in 2003.[42] Due to increased stability in Iraq, Bush announced the withdrawal of additional American forces.[42] This reflected an emerging consensus between the White House and the Pentagon that the war had "turned a corner".[42] He also described what he saw as the success of the 2007 troop surge.[42]
