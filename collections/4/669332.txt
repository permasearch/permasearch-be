The restaurant reopened on 12 March 2009.[43] The cause of the illness was later given by the Health Protection Agency as norovirus, which was thought to originate from oysters which had been harvested from beds contaminated with sewage.[44] The virus was spread further after being contracted by staff members.[45] The restaurant was criticised for its cleaning methods and its slow response to the incident.[44] Complaints of illness from customers totalled 529.[44]
