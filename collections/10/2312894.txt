Among the teaching staff were numerous artists, including Will Hicok Low, who taught from 1889 to 1892. The famous American poet William Cullen Bryant also gave lectures. Architect Alexander Jackson Davis (A.J. Davis) taught at the Academy. Painter Lemuel Wilmarth was the first full-time instructor[5] Gulian C. Verplanck, a Congressman and a man of letters, gave an address at the school in 1824.[6] and Silas Dustin was a curator.[7]
