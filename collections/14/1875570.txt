During World War I she worked for the Allies in Italy as a nurse. This experience inspiring her to create the Anzac Medal in 1916.[4] The medal had the inscription, "Anzac. In Eternal Remembrance, 1914-18." It was a tribute to the "immortal bravery of the Australasians." The first issue of 500 medals was available at two guineas each. The funds raised were devoted to the needs of Australians and New Zealanders permanently disabled in the war.[4][8] There are few of these medals now extant and these are in major institutions.[7]
