In 2006, Dunst collaborated with Sofia Coppola again and starred as the title character in Coppola's historical drama Marie Antoinette, based on Antonia Fraser's book Marie Antoinette: The Journey.[62][63] The movie was screened at a special presentation at the 2006 Cannes Film Festival,[64] and was reviewed favourably.[65] International revenues were $45 million out of $60 million overall.[66]
