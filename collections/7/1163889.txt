On 10 November 2016, the Reserve Bank of India announced a new redesigned ₹100 banknote to be available as a part of the Mahatma Gandhi New Series, in the coming months.[3] On 19 July 2018, the Reserve Bank of India unveiled the revised design of ₹100 banknote.[4] The new note colored its base as a lavender with motif of "Rani ki Vav" (Queen's stepwell) on the reverse side. Located in Gujarat's Patan, "Rani ki Vav" is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
