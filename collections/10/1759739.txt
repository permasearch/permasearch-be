The word "hoosier" is still used in Greater St. Louis to denote a "yokel" or "white trash".[16] The word is also encountered in sea shanties. In the book Shanties from the Seven Seas[17] by Stan Hugill, in reference to its former use to denote cotton-stowers, who would move bales of cotton to and from the holds of ships and force them in tightly by means of jackscrews. "To hoosier" is sometimes still encountered as a verb meaning "to trick" or "to swindle".[citation needed]
