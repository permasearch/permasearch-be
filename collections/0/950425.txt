Many in the investment industry believe the development of ESG factors as considerations in investment analysis to be inevitable.[17] The evidence toward a relationship between consideration for ESG issues and financial performance is becoming greater and the combination of fiduciary duty and a wide recognition of the necessity of the sustainability of investments in the long term has meant that environmental social and corporate governance concerns are now becoming increasingly important in the investment market.[18][2] ESG has become less a question of philanthropy than practicality.
