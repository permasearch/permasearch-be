Syndicated columnist Walter Winchell wrote for the Daily Mirror that a star had been born who would save Broadway from the slump in ticket sales caused by the 1939 New York World's Fair. Winchell's praise of Carmen and her Bando da Lua was repeated on his Blue Network radio show, which reached 55 million listeners daily.[38] The press called Miranda "the girl who saved Broadway from the World's Fair".[39] Her fame grew quickly, and she was formally presented to President Franklin D.
