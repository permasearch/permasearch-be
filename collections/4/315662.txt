Canadian authors have accumulated numerous international awards.[106] In 1992, Michael Ondaatje became the first Canadian to win the Man Booker Prize for The English Patient.[107] Margaret Atwood won the Booker in 2000 for The Blind Assassin[108] and Yann Martel won it in 2002 for the Life of Pi.[109] Carol Shields's The Stone Diaries won the Governor General's Awards in Canada in 1993, the 1995 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction, and the 1994 National Book Critics Circle Award.[110] In 2013, Alice Munro was the first Canadian to be awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for her work as "master of the modern short story".[111] Munro is also a recipient of the Man Booker International Prize for her lifetime body of work, and three-time winner of Canada's Governor General's Award for fiction.[112]
