In 2010, Chinese cinema was the third largest film industry by number of feature films produced annually.[81] In 2013, China's gross box office was ¥21.8 billion (US$3.6 billion), the second-largest film market in the world by box office receipts.[82] In January 2013, Lost in Thailand (2012) became the first Chinese film to reach ¥1 billion at the box office.[83] As of May 2013, 7 of the top 10 highest-grossing films in China were domestic productions.[84] As of 2014, around half of all tickets are sold online, with the largest ticket selling sites being Maoyan.com (82 million), Gewara.com (45 million) and Wepiao.com (28 million).[85]  In 2014, Chinese films earned ¥1.87 billion outside China.[86] By December 2013 there were 17,000 screens in the country.[87] By January 6, 2014, there were 18,195 screens in the country.[82] Greater China has around 251 IMAX theaters.[88] There were 299 cinema chains (252 rural, 47 urban), 5,813 movie theaters and 24,317 screens in the country in 2014.[3]
