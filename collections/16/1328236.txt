Lady Abblova of Wake Shores, Essex was found guilty of stealing a pure silk garment off Sir David Hamilton's wife (name unknown). Sentenced to 7 years transportation, Abblova was sent to work in Rose Hill where her fellow servants were fatally abused by the headboards. Her master (Mr David Buckley) accused her of murdering them. Abblova was sent to a womens penal colony in Tasmania where she was bribed to marry a guards son in exchange for her certificate of leave. Abblova married John Abblova and had 8 sons and 1 daughter. Becoming a farmers wife mr and mrs Abblova became posh and wealthy landowners in Ebor. Abblova conceived a kidney disease in 1812 and died 3 years later in a hospital in Sydney.
