Multiple chapters sprouted nationwide following the example by the original chapter in Chicago with several loose branches forming in NYC, and along the East Coast. National headquarters in Chicago then asked a loose coalition of chapters in New York to form one regional branch. These all accepted neighborhood empowerment and Puerto Rican self-determination as the unifying mission.[4] The national office headed by Jose Cha Cha Jimenez, gave approval because before the 1950's New York was the port of entry for immigrants and Puerto Ricans entering the continental U.S.
