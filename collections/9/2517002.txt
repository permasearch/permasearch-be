In a rematch of the 2013 Big Ten Championship Game, Ohio State defeated the Michigan State Spartans 49–37 in East Lansing.[85][87] Ohio State received the opening kickoff and drove 45 yards in nine plays and failed to score after a missed field goal from Sean Nuernberger.[88] Michigan State scored the first points of the game on their first drive off of a 15-yard touchdown pass from Connor Cook to Keith Mumphery.[85] The Buckeyes responded on their ensuing possession, tying the game at 7–7 after a touchdown run from J.T. Barrett.[88] A 33-yard run from Jeremy Langford on the Spartans' next possession, gave Michigan State a 14–7 lead.[85]
