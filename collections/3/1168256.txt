On March 17, 2012, Sony Pictures announced that it was pursuing a sequel to the film, signing a deal that would see Hill and Bacall return to write a script treatment that would be again developed by Bacall. Hill and Tatum returned to star in the film. They were executive producers as well, alongside producer Neal H. Moritz.[35] Phil Lord and Christopher Miller returned to direct this sequel. The film was originally scheduled to be released on June 6, 2014.[36] On May 8, 2013, it was announced that the film would be pushed back a week until June 13, 2014.[37] The film's title was 22 Jump Street.[38] Like the first film, 22 Jump Street received positive reviews.
