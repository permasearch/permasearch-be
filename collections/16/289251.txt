Shortly after the release of the book, it was announced that Steven Spielberg's DreamWorks Television would be developing a cable miniseries based on the novel. Spielberg and King were announced as executive producers.[39] Showtime was to broadcast the miniseries.[40] Brian K. Vaughan was hired to adapt the book.[41] On November 29, 2012 it was announced that a 13-episode adaptation of Under the Dome would premiere on CBS in the summer of 2013. Under the Dome was an instant success for the network; the premiere in June 2013 broke the record as the most-watched summer drama premiere on any television network since 1992, with the "Pilot" episode reaching over 13 million views. Vaughan wrote the first episode, which was directed by Niels Arden Oplev. Neal Baer will serve as showrunner and executive producer along with Stephen King, Darryl Frank, Justin Falvey, Brian K. Vaughan and Stacey Snider.[42] It premiered on June 24, 2013.[43] A second 13-episode season was announced with Stephen King writing the first episode. The finale of season 2 aired to 7.1 million viewers on September 22, 2014, leaving the story open for a third season which was renewed on October 8, 2014.[44] The show concluded on the 10th of September 2015.
