According to Acclaimed Music, The Stone Roses is the 58th most ranked record on critics' all-time lists.[36] In 1997, it was named the second greatest album of all time in a "Music of the Millennium" poll[37] conducted by HMV, Channel 4, The Guardian and Classic FM. In 1998, Q magazine readers placed it at number 4,[38] while in 2000 the same magazine placed it at number 29 in its list of the "100 Greatest British Albums Ever."[39] In 2004, the album was voted the best British album of all time in The Observer's poll of 100 musicians and critics.[40] In 2006, Q placed the album at number 5 in its list of "40 Best Albums of the '80s".[41] In 2008, it was named the fifth "greatest British album ever" by a Q magazine/HMV poll.[42]
