The album received the largest initial certification of any album for 6× platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America(RIAA) on January 18, 1993.[46] The record was broken by 'N SYNC's No Strings Attached, certified 7× Platinum initially in April 2000.[47] On March 16, 1999, when the RIAA launched the Diamond Awards, honoring sales of 10 million copies or more of an album or single, the album received the award with 62 other albums initially.[48][49] It was certified 17× Platinum by the RIAA on November 1, 1999, becoming the best-selling soundtrack album of all-time in United States.[50][51][52] According to a new update from Whitney Houston's estate, particularly, Arista, The Bodyguard soundtrack has been certified 18x Platinum by RIAA on November 2017[53]. It is the first album to reach both the 10 million and 11 million sales mark in the US since 1991, when Nielsen SoundScan started tracking music sales.[54] As of October 2014, it has sold 12,140,000 copies; it is the sixth best-selling album of the SoundScan era in the United States.[55]
