In January 1995, The River Wild director Curtis Hanson was reported to have been in the process of final negotiations with Scott Rudin and Paramount Pictures to direct The Alienist.[25] By May 1995, Hanson was signed on as the film's director.[26] Variety reported that the film's producers planned for filming to start in Spring 1995, with an intended Christmas release date in mind.[25] Tony Award-winning playwright David Henry Hwang adapted the book for film.[25] Scouts for the film visited Philadelphia in May 1995 to consider location shooting in the city.[26] Film scouts for The Alienist were particularly interested in Philadelphia's Academy of Music, Eastern State Penitentiary, and Waterworks.[26]
