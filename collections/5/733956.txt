McAdams is an environmentalist. She ran an eco-friendly lifestyle website GreenIsSexy.org with two of her friends for five years, from 2007 to 2011.[203] Her house is powered by Bullfrog renewable energy.[204] She travels around Toronto by bicycle and does not own a car,[205] but drives when in Los Angeles because it is "a harder town to cycle in".[204] She volunteered in Biloxi, Mississippi and Louisiana[206] in fall of 2005, as part of the clean-up effort following Hurricane Katrina.[207] McAdams sat on a TreeHugger/Live Earth judging panel in 2007.[208] She appealed for donations during the Canada for Haiti telethon in 2010.[209] She was involved in Matter of Trust's "hair boom" efforts following the 2010 Gulf of Mexico oil spill.[210][211] In 2011, McAdams supported Foodstock, a protest against a proposed limestone mega quarry in Melancthon, Ontario.[212][213] In 2013, she filmed two promotional videos for the Food & Water First Movement, aiming to preserve prime farmland and source water in Ontario, Canada.[214][215] In 2014, she narrated the feature documentary Take Me To The River, which investigates what is being done to try to save iconic rivers.[216][217]
