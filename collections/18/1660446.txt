On 7 October 2012, Libya's Prime Minister-elect Mustafa A.G. Abushagur was ousted after failing a second time to win parliamentary approval for a new cabinet.[95][96][97] On 14 October 2012, the General National Congress elected former GNC member and human rights lawyer Ali Zeidan as prime minister-designate.[98] Zeidan was sworn in after his cabinet was approved by the GNC.[99][100] On 11 March 2014, after having been ousted by the GNC for his inability to halt a rogue oil shipment,[101] Prime Minister Zeiden stepped down, and was replaced by Prime Minister Abdullah al-Thani.[102] On 25 March 2014, in the face of mounting instability, al-Thani's government briefly explored the possibility of the restoration of the Libyan monarchy.[103]
