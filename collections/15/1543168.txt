On the morning of Friday, 19 May, Anne Boleyn was executed within the Tower precincts, not upon the site of the execution memorial, but rather, according to historian Eric Ives, on a scaffold erected on the north side of the White Tower, in front of what is now the Waterloo Barracks.[127] She wore a red petticoat under a loose, dark grey gown of damask trimmed in fur and a mantle of ermine.[128] Accompanied by two female attendants, Anne made her final walk from the Queen's House to the scaffold and she showed a "devilish spirit"[129] and looked "as gay as if she was not going to die".[130] Anne climbed the scaffold and made a short speech to the crowd:
