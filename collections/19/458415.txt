Uncertainty was a major factor, argued by several economists, that contributed to the worsening and length of the depression. It was also said to be responsible “for the initial decline in consumption that marks the” beginning of the Great Depression by economists Paul R. Flacco and Randall E. Parker. Economist Ludwig Lachmann argues that it was pessimism that prevented the recovery and worsening of the depression[101] President Hoover is said to have been blinded from what was right in front of him.
