Named after the character,[176] the "Rachel" refers to a bouncy layered[177] shag inspired by the way in which Aniston wore her hair on Friends between 1994 and 1996, during the first and second seasons of the series.[162] The "Rachel" debuted in the show's 20th episode, "The One With the Evil Orthodontist".[178] Aniston believes that her hair stylist, Chris McMillan, created the haircut while he was under the influence of marijuana.[179] The "Rachel" immediately became popular among women, launching an international hair trend.[180] The popularity of the "Rachel" coincided with the popularity of Friends during the mid-to-late-1990s.[181] Marie Claire estimates that 11 million women donned the hairstyle throughout the decade,[182] while the Daily Express determined that the hairstyle was most popular among British women,[183] who went to hair salons "clutching magazine pictures of Aniston" and asking hairdressers to give them the look.[184]
