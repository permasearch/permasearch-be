Patton's first posting was with the 15th Cavalry at Fort Sheridan, Illinois,[24] where he established himself as a hard-driving leader who impressed superiors with his dedication.[25] In late 1911, Patton was transferred to Fort Myer, Virginia, where many of the Army's senior leaders were stationed. Befriending Secretary of War Henry L. Stimson, Patton served as his aide at social functions on top of his regular duties as quartermaster for his troop.[26]
