Japan ranked fourth in the world in 1996 in tonnage of fish caught.[141] Japan captured 4,074,580 metric tons of fish in 2005, down from 4,987,703 tons in 2000, 9,558,615 tons in 1990, 9,864,422 tons in 1980, 8,520,397 tons in 1970, 5,583,796 tons in 1960 and 2,881,855 tons in 1950.[142] In 2003, the total aquaculture production was predicted at 1,301,437 tonnes.[143] In 2010, Japan's total fisheries production was 4,762,469 fish.[144] Offshore fisheries accounted for an average of 50% of the nation's total fish catches in the late 1980s although they experienced repeated ups and downs during that period.
