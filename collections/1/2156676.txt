When the Turkic peoples began their late medieval conquest of Asia Minor, Byzantine Greek citizens were the largest group of indigenous peoples living there.[25] Even after the Turkic conquests of the interior, the mountainous Black Sea coast of Asia Minor remained the heart of a Greek state, the Empire of Trebizond, until its eventual conquest by the Ottoman Turks in 1461, nearly a decade after the fall of the European territory of the Greeks to the Ottomans.
