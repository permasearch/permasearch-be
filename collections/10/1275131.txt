After graduation, Hughes worked at the Veterans Administration for a few weeks before moving on to the state department.[3] In 1930, Hughes worked at the US Department of Agriculture as a research clerk then as a scientific illustrator and translator.[1] She attained stature as an artist and scholar during years of government service. She used her fluency in four languages- French, Spanish, Portuguese and Italian- to work as a translator in the State department during World War I.[4] Also at the departments of war and commerce where her most prestigious assignment was translating during the Conference on limitation of armament in Washington, after the armistice.[1] She served as a translator for the US department of State, one of only two non-French women employed by the US office of Translation.[2]
