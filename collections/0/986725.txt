The New York Times noted on January 3 that the new pieces had been received by the Sub-Treasury and would be released two to a customer, starting on January 9.  It stated that the Mint was working as hard as possible to keep up with demand, but that initially quantities would be limited.[46] Banking, the journal of the American Bankers Association, stated that "The designs of the new coins have been highly praised by those having expert knowledge of such matters."[47]  A Connecticut newspaper predicted readers would like the new half dollar five times as much as the new dime.[48]
