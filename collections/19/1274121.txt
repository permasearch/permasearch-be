In February 2014 the European Parliament passed regulations requiring standardization and quality control for liquids and vaporizers, disclosure of ingredients in liquids, and child-proofing and tamper-proofing for liquid packaging.[36][239] In April 2014 the FDA published proposed regulations for e-cigarettes along similar lines.[240][241] In the US some states tax e-cigarettes as tobacco products, and some state and regional governments have broadened their indoor smoking bans to include e-cigarettes.[63] As of 9 October 2015, at least 48 states and 2 territories banned e-cigarette sales to minors.[242]
