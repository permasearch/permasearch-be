At the time, she was 20 years old and dancing professionally with American Ballet Theatre's Studio Company (then known as ABT II) and when it came time to decide if she would move to the main company, Baryshnikov who was the Artistic Director at the time told her "You're a lovely dancer, but you're too stocky." In her words, that was "my epiphany when I decided to stop dancing and not be a ballet dancer. It was a wonderful moment because I could've been banging my head against the wall for another 10 years."[5]
