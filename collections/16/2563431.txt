In 1934, Mountbatten was appointed to his first command – the destroyer HMS Daring.[8] His ship was a new destroyer, which he was to sail to Singapore and exchange for an older ship, HMS Wishart.[8] He successfully brought Wishart back to port in Malta and then attended the funeral of King George V in January 1936.[21] Mountbatten was appointed a Personal Naval Aide-de-Camp to King Edward VIII on 23 June 1936,[22] and, having joined the Naval Air Division of the Admiralty in July 1936,[1] he attended the coronation of King George VI and Queen Elizabeth in May 1937.[23] He was promoted Captain on 30 June 1937[24] and was then given command of the destroyer HMS Kelly in June 1939.[25]
