The 117th annual Boston Marathon was run on Patriots' Day, April 15, 2013. At 2:49 p.m. EDT (18:49 UTC), two bombs detonated about 210 yards (190 m) apart at the finish line on Boylston Street near Copley Square.[18][19][20][21] The first exploded outside Marathon Sports at 671–673 Boylston Street at 2:49:43 p.m.[18] At the time of the first explosion, the race clock at the finish line showed 04:09:43,[22] reflecting the elapsed time since the Wave 3 start at 10:40 a.m. The second bomb exploded at 2:49:57 p.m.,[19][23] about 12 seconds later and one block farther west at 755 Boylston Street.[6] The explosions took place nearly three hours after the winner crossed the finish line,[23] but with more than 5,700 runners yet to finish.[24]
