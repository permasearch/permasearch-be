Though his career spanned the desegregation and reintegration of the NFL, as an owner, he never had an African American on any of his teams, but Bert Jr. believed the mere discussion of whether his father was prejudiced was absurd.[100] Bell's handling of the merger with the AAFC was acclaimed as a personal triumph.[173] Although he did not have the wherewithal to prevent the wholesale betting on games,[174] he was proactive in ensuring games were not tampered with by gamblers,[175] and he created the foundation of the contemporary NFL anti-gambling policy.[176]
