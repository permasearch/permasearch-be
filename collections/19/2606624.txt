On February 9, 2007, Marvel producer Avi Arad announced the development of Ghost Rider 2 at a press event.[18] Peter Fonda had also expressed a desire to return as Mephistopheles.[19] In early December, 2007, Nicolas Cage also expressed interest to return in the lead role as Ghost Rider.[20] Shortly after, in another interview he went on further to mention that he would enjoy seeing a darker story, adding, "He's not eating jelly beans anymore; he's getting drunk". He suggested that the film could do with newly created villains.[21] It was also rumored that the sequel would feature Danny Ketch, another Marvel character who took up the Ghost Rider mantle in the comics.[22]
