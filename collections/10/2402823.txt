Fang states, “[W]hen the two come into conflict, it surely will cause great confrontation and if the power of the community is strong enough, it often results in the destruction of the individual.”[11] Readers see throughout the course of the story that the members of the community begrudgingly stay civil with Emily. None of the members of the community truly care for her. They look down on her and her traditional way of life. This creates this conflict for Emily in her life.[12]
