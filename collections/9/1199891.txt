Rotten Tomatoes gives it an aggregate rating of 15% based on 123 reviews and an average rating of 3.7/10. The site's consensus reads that the film is a "scattershot comedy that's silly and obvious".[7] On Metacritic, the film has a rating of 41 out of 100, based on 31 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".[8] Audiences polled by CinemaScore gave the film an average grade of "B+" on an A+ to F scale.[9] Film critic Richard Roeper put the film at #1 on his list of the worst films of 2004 amongst claims of unconvincing prosthetics and racism. It was nominated for five Razzies, including Worst Picture, Worst Actress for the Wayans brothers in drag, Worst Director, Worst Screenplay and Worst Screen Couple, but failed to get any of those awards.[10]
