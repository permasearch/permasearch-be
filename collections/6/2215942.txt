The United States and Vietnam had a relations during World War II when a group of American agents of the OSS, the predecessor of CIA, landed in Vietnam and gathered with the future leader of North Vietnam, Ho Chi Minh, who was the leader of the Viet Minh.[6] The agent group, led by Allison Thomas, had co-operated together in French Indochina against Japan. The Viet Minh had given shelters for the American agents.
