The 1989 census recorded 341,000 ethnic Russians, or 6.3 percent of the population,[209] 52,000 Ukrainians and 100,000 Greeks in Georgia.[210] Since 1990, 1.5 million Georgian nationals have left.[210] At least 1 million emigrants from Georgia legally or illegally reside in Russia.[211] Georgia's net migration rate is −4.54, excluding Georgian nationals who live abroad.[citation needed] Georgia has nonetheless been inhabited by immigrants from all over the world throughout its independence. According to 2014 statistics, Georgia gets most of its immigrants from Russia, 51.6 percent of all immigrants.[212][n 1]
