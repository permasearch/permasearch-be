In June 2011, Joby Harold was hired to rework the screenplay.[12] By September, Warner approached Brad Pitt to star;[13] after he declined, the studio then approached Tom Cruise.[14] Once Cruise accepted, the script changed the age of the leading role to fit the actor's. In December 2011, Cruise officially joined the film.[15] Emily Blunt entered negotiations to star opposite Cruise in April 2012.[16] Screenwriting duo Roberto Orci and Alex Kurtzman also delivered a draft of their own.[17]
