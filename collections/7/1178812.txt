Treatment may include switching to an antipsychotic with a lower risk of the condition.[2] Medications with tentative evidence of benefit include diphenhydramine, trazodone, benztropine, mirtazapine, and beta blockers.[3][2] Vitamin B6 or correcting iron deficiency may also be useful.[3][2] Around half of people on antipsychotics develop the condition.[5] The term was first used by Ladislav Haškovec, who described the phenomenon in 1901.[6] It is from Greek a- meaning "not" and καθίζειν kathízein meaning "to sit" or in other words an "inability to sit".[2]
