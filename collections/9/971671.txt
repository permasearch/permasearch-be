Homer's influence on comedy and culture has been significant. In 2010, Entertainment Weekly named Homer "the greatest character of the last 20 years."[82] He was placed second on TV Guide's 2002 Top 50 Greatest Cartoon Characters, behind Bugs Bunny;[83] fifth on Bravo's 100 Greatest TV Characters, one of only four cartoon characters on that list;[84] and first in a Channel 4 poll of the greatest television characters of all time.[85] In 2007, Entertainment Weekly placed Homer ninth on their list of the "50 Greatest TV icons" and first on their 2010 list of the "Top 100 Characters of the Past Twenty Years".[86][87][88] Homer was also the runaway winner in British polls that determined who viewers thought was the "greatest American"[89] and which fictional character people would like to see become the President of the United States.[90] His relationship with Marge was included in TV Guide's list of "The Best TV Couples of All Time".[91]
