On 8 October 2015, the Republic of Ireland beat world champions Germany 1–0 in a Euro 2016 qualifier at the Aviva Stadium. Shane Long scored the game's only goal with an excellent finish[42] in the 70th minute, rewarding the Republic of Ireland's impressive defensive display.[43] The result, hailed as one of the Republic of Ireland's greatest,[44] guaranteed[45] the Republic of Ireland a play-off place at least, with hopes of automatic qualification still a reality going into the final group game against Poland in Warsaw.[46] A win, or a draw of 2–2 or more,[45] would guarantee at least second place in the group and ensure automatic qualification[47] for the finals in France. The Republic of Ireland, however, lost 2–1,[48] thus entering them into the play-offs.[49]
