The Yugozapaden planning area is the most developed region with a per capita gross domestic product (PPP) of $26,580 in 2016.[204] It includes the capital city and the surrounding Sofia Province, which alone generate 42 per cent of national gross domestic product.[205] PPP GDP per capita and the cost of living in 2017 stood at 49 and 48.4 per cent of the EU average, respectively.[206][207] National PPP GDP was estimated at $143.1 billion in 2016, with a per capita value of $20,116.[208] Economic growth statistics take into account illegal transactions from the informal economy, which is the largest in the EU as a percentage of economic output.[209][210] The Bulgarian National Bank issues the national currency, lev, which is pegged to the euro at a rate of 1.95583 levа per euro.[211]
