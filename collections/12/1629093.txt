In 1972 and 1973, Mays was the oldest regular position player in baseball. At age 42, he became the oldest position player to appear in a World Series game.[43] Mays retired after the 1973 season with a lifetime batting average of .302 and 660 home runs. His lifetime total of 7,095 outfield putouts remains the major league record.[44] Mays is the only major league player to have hit a home run in every inning from the 1st through the 16th innings.[45] He finished his career with a record 22 extra-inning home runs.
