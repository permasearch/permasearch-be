While the Port-au-Prince airport ramp has spaces for more than a dozen airliners, in the days following the quake, it sometimes served nearly 40 at once, creating serious delays.[171][172] The supply backup at the airport was expected to ease as the apron management improved, and when the perceived need for heavy security diminished.[98] Airport congestion was reduced on 18 January when the United Nations and US forces formally agreed to prioritise humanitarian flights over security reinforcement.[173]
