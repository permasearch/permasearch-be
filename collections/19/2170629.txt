When Trump took office in January 2017, the United States were involved in the War in Afghanistan since 2001, the longest war in American history.[513] The US then had 8,400 American troops in Afghanistan. Most of them participated in the NATO mission Resolute Support, intended to train and advise the Afghan government troops (in their (civil) war against Taliban, Al-Qaeda and ISIL-in-Khorasan[514]); 2,000 American troops were charged with fighting against terror groups such as ISIL-in-Khorasan.[515][516] By August 2017, the American force in Afghanistan was estimated at 10,000 troops.[517] On August 21, 2017, Trump announced expansion of the American presence in Afghanistan, without giving details on how.[518]
