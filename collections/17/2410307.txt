Claire Headley describes how staff lived in constant paranoia due to being required to submit "knowledge reports" on each other if they heard any critical statements or casual asides. Becoming the subject of a report meant that the accused person was interrogated and made to recant or publicly confess their "crimes" against Scientology. Janet Reitman comments that "everyone at the Int Base lived in fear of everyone else and what they might be saying, or reporting, about one another."[71]
