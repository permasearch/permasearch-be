Confirmation hearings before the Senate Foreign Relations Committee began on January 13, 2009, a week before the Obama inauguration; two days later, the committee voted 16–1 to approve Clinton.[27]  By this time, Clinton's public approval rating had reached 65 percent, the highest point since the Lewinsky scandal.[28] On January 21, 2009, Clinton was confirmed in the full Senate by a roll call vote of 94–2.[29] Clinton took the oath of office of Secretary of State and resigned from the Senate the same day.[30]
