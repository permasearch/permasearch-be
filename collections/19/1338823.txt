The Mohawk name in modern orthography as standardized in 1993 is Kaniatarakwà:ronte, meaning bulging lake.[citation needed] An alternate name is Kaniá:tare tsi kahnhokà:ronte (phonetic English spelling Caniaderi Guarunte[14] ), meaning 'door of the country' or 'lake to the country'. The lake is an important eastern gateway to Iroquois Confederacy lands.
