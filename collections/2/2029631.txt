At 1,500 feet (460 m), the walk was the highest of Wallenda's career, about seven times as high as the Niagara crossing.[91]  He covered a distance of approximately 1,400 feet (430 m) in 22 minutes, 54 seconds, using a 2-inch-thick (5.1 cm) wire.[13][91]  He carried a 30-foot-long (9.1 m) balancing pole weighing 43 pounds (20 kg).[97]
