According to the 2011 census, 61.47% were Hindus, 34.22% were Muslims.[75][76] Christian minorities (3.7%) are found among the Scheduled Tribe population.[77] The scheduled Tribe population in Assam is around 13%, of which Bodos account for 40%.[78] Other religions followed include Jainism (0.1%), Buddhism (0.2%), Sikhism (0.1%) and Animism (amongst Khamti, Phake, Aiton etc. communities). Many Hindus in Assam are followers of the Ekasarana Dharma sect of Hinduism, which gave rise to Namghar, designed to be simpler places of worship than traditional Hindu temples.[citation needed]
