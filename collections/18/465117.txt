St Paul's idea of redemption hinged upon the contrast between the sin of Adam and the death and resurrection of Jesus. "Therefore, just as sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people, because all sinned."[15] "For as in Adam all die, so in Christ all will be made alive."[16] Up till then the transgression in the Garden of Eden had not been given great significance.
