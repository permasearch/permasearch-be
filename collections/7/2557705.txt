At the methodeutical level Peirce held that a hypothesis is judged and selected[18] for testing because it offers, via its trial, to expedite and economize the inquiry process itself toward new truths, first of all by being testable and also by further economies,[20] in terms of cost, value, and relationships among guesses (hypotheses).
