According to section 2 of the Regency Act 1937, the people who can make a declaration of incapacity (or a declaration that the incapacity has ended) are the consort of the Sovereign, the Lord Chancellor, the Speaker of the House of Commons, the Lord Chief Justice of England and the Master of the Rolls. As of  November 2017[update] these positions were held by, respectively, Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh, David Gauke, John Bercow, Lord Burnett of Maldon, and Sir Terrence Etherton.
