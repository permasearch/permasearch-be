The soundtrack for Evita was released in the United States on November 12, 1996.[28] Warner Bros. Records released two versions: a two-disc edition entitled Evita: The Complete Motion Picture Music Soundtrack, which featured all the tracks from the film,[49] and Evita: Music from the Motion Picture, a single-disc edition.[50] AllMusic's Stephen Thomas Erlewine described the soundtrack as "unengaging",[51] while Hartford Courant's Greg Morago praised Madonna's singing abilities.[52] The soundtrack was a commercial success, reaching the top of the charts in Austria, Belgium, Scotland, Switzerland and the United Kingdom.[53][54][55][56] According to Carol Clerk's book Madonna Style, the soundtrack has sold 11 million copies worldwide.[57]
