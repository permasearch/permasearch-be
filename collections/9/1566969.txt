Public expenditure health was at 8.9% of the GDP in 2004, whereas private expenditure was at 1.3%.[57] As of 2012[update], the HIV/AIDS prevalence was at 2.8% among 15- to 49-year-olds.[6] Health expenditure was at US$30 per capita in 2004.[57] A large proportion of the population is undernourished,[57] with malnutrition being a problem in Congo-Brazzaville.[58] There were 20 physicians per 100,000 persons in the early 2000s (decade).[57]
