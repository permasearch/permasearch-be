The University of Central Florida is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools according to the U.S. Department of Education.[62] UCF's academic calendar is based on the semester system,[63] with the typical fall semester running from the end of August until the beginning of December, and the typical spring semester running from the beginning of January through the beginning of May. In addition, UCF offers four different summer semesters, A, B, C, and D, ranging from six weeks to twelve weeks.[64] The Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching classifies UCF as a "large four-year, primarily nonresidential" university with a "comprehensive doctoral" graduate instructional program and "highest research activity."[65]
