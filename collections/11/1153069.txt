In the 1980s under the chief executive officer (CEO) Kari Kairamo, Nokia expanded, mostly through acquisitions. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, however, Nokia corporation experienced financial difficulties. Losses were made in the television manufacturing division.[34] In 1988, Nokian Tyres became a separate entity.[35] Nokia's paper division was sold in 1989. In 1990, Nokian Footwear was a separate entity.[4]. During the rest of the 1990s, Nokia divested itself of all other businesses.[36]
