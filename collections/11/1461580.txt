Throughout the period of the Cold War, titanium was considered a strategic material by the U.S. government, and a large stockpile of titanium sponge was maintained by the Defense National Stockpile Center, which was finally depleted in the 2000s.[50] According to 2006 data, the world's largest producer, Russian-based VSMPO-AVISMA, was estimated to account for about 29% of the world market share.[51] As of 2015, titanium sponge metal was produced in six countries: China, Japan, Russia, Kazakhstan, the US, Ukraine, and India.
