In early 2015, leading up to Australia Day, opposition leader Bill Shorten called for a new push for Australia to become a republic.[19] Former ARM chair Malcolm Turnbull said upon his appointment as prime minister in September of the same year he would not pursue "his dream" of Australia becoming a republic until after the end of the Queen's reign, instead focusing his efforts toward the economy.[20] A majority of parliamentarians currently support Australia becoming a republic.[21]
