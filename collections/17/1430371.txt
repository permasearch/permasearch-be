The Jewish Autonomous Oblast continues to be an autonomous oblast of the Russian state.[118] The Chief Rabbi of Birobidzhan, Mordechai Scheiner, says there are 4,000 Jews in the capital city.[119] Governor Nikolay Mikhaylovich Volkov has stated that he intends to "support every valuable initiative maintained by our local Jewish organizations".[120] The Birobidzhan Synagogue opened in 2004 on the 70th anniversary of the regions founding in 1934.[121]
