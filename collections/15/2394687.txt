On December 19, 1961, a two-mile (3.2 km) segment of the Capital Beltway (I-495) was opened to traffic between Georgia Avenue (MD 97) and University Boulevard East (MD 193).[23][24] On Monday, August 17, 1964, the final segment of the 64-mile (103 km) Beltway was opened to traffic,[25] and a ribbon-cutting ceremony was held near the New Hampshire Avenue interchange, with a speech by then-Gov. J. Millard Tawes,[26] who called it a "road of opportunity" for Maryland and the nation.[27]
