The most immediate result of the protests was seen in increased internet freedoms.[102] While commentators were divided about the extent to which the internet contributed to the ousting of Ben Ali from power,[103][104] Facebook remained accessible to roughly 20% of the population throughout the crisis[104][105] whilst its passwords were hacked by a country-wide man-in-the-middle attack,[106] YouTube and DailyMotion became available after Ben Ali's ouster,[107] and the Tor anonymity network reported a surge of traffic from Tunisia.[108]
