The song debuted at number two on the UK Singles Chart, behind Westlife's cover of Barry Manilow's "Mandy".[41] Coincidentally, both bands were managed by Louis Walsh at the time.[42] "Jump" fell just two places the following week.[43] The single spent four more weeks in the top twenty before falling. Overall, the song managed to spend a total of fourteen weeks in the top 75.[44] It was certified silver by the British Phonographic Industry.[45]
