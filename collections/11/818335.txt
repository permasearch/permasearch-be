On the UK album chart, it debuted at No. 2 with 24,809 copies, while the No. 1 album that week — Michael Bublé's To Be Loved — debuted with five times as many copies.[54][55] It was later certified Gold by the British Phonographic Industry for 100,000 copies shipped. On the Canadian Albums Chart, Save Rock and Roll debuted at No. 1 with 8,000 first week sales,[56][57] and later achieved Gold status for 40,000 shipments. It opened at No. 2 in both Australia and New Zealand.
