Security guard Melvin Dismukes, who was black, was the first to be charged.[31] He was arraigned for the felonious assault of James Sortor and Michael Clark in the first-floor hallway of the annex. He was freed on $1,500 bail. Dismukes's trial took place in May 1968. He was found not guilty of the charge of felonious assault.[32][33] The all-white jury returned the verdict in 13 minutes.[34]
