The 19th century saw at least 250,000 Jews convert to Christianity according to existing records of various societies.[157] Data from the Pew Research Center has it that, as of 2013, about 1.6 million adult American Jews identify themselves as Christians, most as Protestants.[158][159][160] According to the same data, most of the Jews who identify themselves as some sort of Christian (1.6 million) were raised as Jew] or are Jews by ancestry.[159] According to a 2012 study, 17% of Jews in Russia identify themselves as Christians.[161][162]
