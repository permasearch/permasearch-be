Tisdale told MTV in 2013 that she had begun working on her third studio album.[85] She said she was inspired to make music again and that she hoped to "surprise people a little bit, something different from what I've done before".[85][86] Tisdale announced to MTV that she opted to pursue a dance-pop sound on the album, which was expected to be released in 2014.[85] In December 2013, Tisdale teased on her Twitter account about a "special project" related to music.[87] She confirmed that the project was a single titled "You're Always Here", which was written about her late grandfather.[87] The song was eventually released independently to digital stores that same month.[88]
