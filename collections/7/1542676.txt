Queen debuted with relatively low collections and grossed ₹20 million (US$310,000) on the first day, grossing ₹33.5 million (US$520,000) on the second day, and ₹43 million (US$670,000) on the third day, taking the first weekend's collection to ₹95 million (US$1.5 million).[35] Despite a poor start, Queen held up well in the first weekdays and grossed around ₹25 million (US$390,000) per day during the week and ended the first week with ₹180 million (US$2.8 million).[36] On its second Monday it grossed ₹30 million (US$470,000), coinciding with a holiday in India.[37] Queen had higher collection in the second week than the first week, collecting ₹390 million (US$6.1 million) in two weeks.[38][39] The movie had a massive growth by far in 2014 for the third week collection by ₹115 million (US$1.8 million), which totals the third week collection to ₹505 million (US$7.9 million).[40] In the fourth week, the film managed a promising ₹65 million (US$1.0 million), which is the 11th highest gross of all time in the history of Hindi cinema for the fourth week. The final domestic collection was just above ₹600 million (US$9.4 million).[41][42]
