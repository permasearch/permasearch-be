In May 2014, BNY Mellon sold its 1 Wall Street headquarters,[52] and in 2015, moved into leased space at Brookfield Place.[53] In June 2014, the company combined its global markets, global collateral services and prime services to create the new Markets Group,[54] also known as BNY Markets Mellon.[55] The company expanded its Hong Kong office in October 2014 as part of the company's plans to grow its wealth management business.[56]
