On September 10, 2017, Hurricane Irma made landfall in Cudjoe Key. The storm destroyed an estimated 25% of the houses on the Keys and another 65% suffered major damage.[14] Most residents had evacuated before the storm hit the area. On September 12, parts of the Keys were still inaccessible by causeway and some areas were closed to the public.[15] Governor Rick Scott reported devastation; most areas were without power or water.[16] The damage was the worst in the Lower Keys, though less severe in Key West;[17] parts of the Lower Keys may be uninhabitable for months.[18]
