Germany invaded Poland on September 1; the USSR followed on September 17. The Soviets quelled opposition by executing and arresting thousands. They sent hundreds of thousands to Siberia and other remote parts of the USSR. Estimates varying from the figure over 1.5 million.[22][23][24][25][26][27][28] to the most conservative figures[29][30][31] using recently found NKVD documents showing 309,000[32][33][34] to 381,220.[34][35] in four major waves of deportations between 1939 and 1941.
