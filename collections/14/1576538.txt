Following Sir Menzies Campbell's resignation on 15 October 2007, Huhne was considered to be one of the strongest contenders for the leadership of the Liberal Democrats.[45] On 17 October, he became the first member of the party to announce his candidacy, saying "I've decided to give it a go" and declaring his vision of a "fairer and greener society".[45] Huhne said that he wanted the party to be committed to the idea that "everybody's individual worth and chance is given its full possibility."[45]
