Nonetheless, share prices of the five largest British banks fell an average of 21% on the morning after the referendum.[62] Shares in many other non-UK banks also fell by more than 10%.[63] By the end of Friday's trading, both HSBC and Standard Chartered had fully recovered, while Lloyds, RBS Group and Barclays remained more than 10% down.[64] All of the Big Three credit rating agencies reacted negatively to the vote: Standard & Poor's cut the UK credit rating from AAA to AA, Fitch Group cut from AA+ to AA, and Moody's cut the UK's outlook to "negative".[65]
