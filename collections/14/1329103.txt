Labh Singh allegedly masterminded[20] what was at that time the largest bank robbery in Indian history, netting almost 60 million (58 million rupees-US$4.5 million) from Millar Ganj branch of Punjab National Bank, Ludhiana;[20] a part of this stolen money belonged to the Reserve Bank of India, India's central bank.[21][22] It was documented as “Biggest Bank Robbery” under “Curiosities and wonders” in Limca Book of Records.[23] The loot enabled the Khalistan Commando Force to buy sophisticated weapons[24] and AK-47 rifles.[25] Sikh militants often used bank robberies to finance their campaign against the Indian government.[21]
