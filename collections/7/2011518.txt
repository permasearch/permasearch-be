The Teme has in recent times often bursts its banks. June and July 2007 saw serious floods in a number of areas, including Leintwardine,[7] Tenbury Wells and Ludlow although the watercourse that flooded the last location was a tributary, the River Corve.[8] However, the upper reaches are also prone to drying out, noted by the Environment Agency to occur every three years in the past 20 years.[9]
