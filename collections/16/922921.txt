The municipality of Venice is subdivided into six administrative Boroughs (MunicipalitÃ ). Each Borough is governed by a Council (Consiglio) and a President, elected contextually[clarification needed] to the city Mayor. The urban organization is governed by the Italian Constitution (art. 114). The Boroughs have the power to advise the Mayor with nonbinding opinions on a large spectrum of topics (environment, construction, public health, local markets) and exercise the functions delegated to them by the City Council; in addition they are supplied with autonomous funding to finance local activities.
