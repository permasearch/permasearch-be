Zappa stated regarding the album's lyrics, "I detest 'love lyrics'."[5] He intentionally wrote lyrics he described as "sub-Mongoloid" to satirize the genre.[5] The music of Cruising with Ruben & the Jets was the most straightforward genre work the Mothers of Invention had performed yet, attempting to faithfully reproduce the sound of 1950s doo wop and rock and roll.[1] However, the arrangements included quotes from Igor Stravinsky pieces and unusual chord changes and tempos.[5][7]
