A preliminary election was held on Tuesday, September 26, 2017 with Walsh and Jackson advancing into a November runoff election.[4] In the November election, Walsh secured a landslide victory, winning 2-1.[5] A total of 108,909 (of the city's 390,136 registered voters) cast a ballot in the November election. The voter turnout of 28%, down 10 percentage points on turnout in the 2013 mayoral election (which generated more excitement as the first Boston mayoral race in a generation without an incumbent).[6]
