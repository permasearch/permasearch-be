The Trust for America's Health ranked Texas 15th highest in adult obesity, with 27.2 percent of the state's population measured as obese.[287] The 2008 Men's Health obesity survey ranked four Texas cities among the top 25 fattest cities in America; Houston ranked 6th, Dallas 7th, El Paso 8th, and Arlington 14th.[288] Texas had only one city, Austin, ranked 21st, in the top 25 among the "fittest cities" in America.[288] The same survey has evaluated the state's obesity initiatives favorably with a "B+".[288] The state is ranked forty-second in the percentage of residents who engage in regular exercise.[289]
