Nevertheless, in a growing number of countries there are now more ex-smokers than smokers [21] For example, in the U.S. as of 2010, there were 47 million ex-smokers and 46 million smokers.[193] In 2014, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reports that the number of adult smokers, 18 years and older, in the U.S. has fallen to 40 million current smokers.[194]
