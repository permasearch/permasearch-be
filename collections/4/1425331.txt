Aerion announced at the May 2014 EBACE that it is redesigning its previous Aerion SBJ with a larger cabin, more range and three yet unspecified engines for a $100 million-plus target price, it aims to fly a prototype in late 2018 / early 2019 and certify the plane in 2021.[3][4] The unit cost was predicted to be US$120 million at the May 2015 EBACE.[5] In 2015 it was scheduled to enter service in 2023.[6] In May 2017, Aerion was hoping to launch it in early 2018.[7]
