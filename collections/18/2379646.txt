Paul V. Kroskrity grew up in Brooklyn. He received his Bachelor's degree from Columbia College, Columbia University[6] where he studied Oriental Studies, Comparative Literature, and Comparative Religion.[5] For graduate school he moved to Indiana University and was the last student of linguist Carl Voegelin.[7] He also worked with Allen D. Grimshaw, Charles S. Bird, and Raymond J. DeMallie. Voegelin inspired his interest in American Indian languages and suggested that he conduct research with the Arizona Tewa.[8]
