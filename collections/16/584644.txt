Because of A&M's military roots, many Aggies have become leaders in the armed forces, and were featured in the 1943 propaganda film We've Never Been Licked.[251] George H. Gay, Jr., was the sole survivor of Torpedo Squadron 8 in the Battle of Midway.[252] Lieutenant General Jay T. Robbins became a fighter ace in World War II with 22 aerial victories.[253] Major General Robert B. Williams led World War II raid on the Schweinfurt ball bearing factories.[254] General Bernard Adolph Schriever, known as "the architect of the Air Force's ballistic missile and military space program", became the namesake of Schriever Air Force Base, Colorado.[255][256] General Michael Moseley is a former Chief of Staff of the United States Air Force.[257]
