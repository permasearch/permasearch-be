The publication of Professor Lord Darzi's review of the NHS[6] prompted criticism of the government and the department of health claiming that it paved the way for user charging,[7] and so contradicting the NHS Plan 2000 which stated that "user charges are unfair and inequitable in they increase the proportion of funding from the unhealthy, old and poor compared with the healthy, young and wealthy".[8] The report also introduces the concept of personal budgets.
