At the age of thirty-two, Damien Chazelle became the youngest person to win Best Director; Norman Taurog was thirty-three when he won Best Director for the 1931 comedy Skippy.[24][25] Kevin O'Connell finally ended the longest losing streak in Oscar history after 20 unsuccessful nominations for sound mixing, winning for Hacksaw Ridge.[26] Moonlight's Dede Gardner became the first woman to win twice for producing, following her previous Best Picture win for 12 Years a Slave.[27]
