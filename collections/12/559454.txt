In November 1956, as a direct result of the Suez Crisis,[72] Syria signed a pact with the Soviet Union. This gave a foothold for Communist influence within the government in exchange for military equipment.[71] Turkey then became worried about this increase in the strength of Syrian military technology, as it seemed feasible that Syria might attempt to retake Ä°skenderun. Only heated debates in the United Nations lessened the threat of war.[73]
