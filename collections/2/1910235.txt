George Bush was widely seen as a "pragmatic caretaker" president who lacked a unified and compelling long-term theme in his efforts.[206][207][208] Indeed, Bush's sound bite where he refers to the issue of overarching purpose as "the vision thing" has become a metonym applied to other political figures accused of similar difficulties.[209][210][211][212][213][214] "He does not say why he wants to be there", wrote columnist George Will, "so the public does not know why it should care if he gets his way".[40]
