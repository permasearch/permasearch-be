When Horst Reichenbach arrived in Athens towards the end of 2011 to head a new European Union task force, the Greek media instantly dubbed him "Third Reichenbach".[217] Almost four million German tourists—more than any other EU country—visit Greece annually, but they comprised most of the 50,000 cancelled bookings in the ten days after the 6 May 2012 Greek elections, a figure The Observer called "extraordinary". The Association of Greek Tourism Enterprises estimates that German visits for 2012 will decrease by about 25%.[241] Such is the ill-feeling, historic claims on Germany from WWII have been reopened,[242] including "a huge, never-repaid loan the nation was forced to make under Nazi occupation from 1941 to 1945."[243]
