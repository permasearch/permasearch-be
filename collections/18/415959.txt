Born on 23 July 1982, Mison was raised in Woking, Surrey, part of the London commuter belt.[3] He attended Hurtwood House, a sixth form college which specialises in drama and music.[4] He trained at the Webber-Douglas Academy, where he was awarded the Sir John Gielgud Trust Award, from 2001 to 2004.[5][6] His speech coach at Webber-Douglas advised him to aim for period pieces.[7][8] A close friend at Webber-Douglas was Rupert Friend, an actor and writer with whom Mison collaborated to create the short film The Continuing and Lamentable Saga of the Suicide Brothers in 2009.[9]
