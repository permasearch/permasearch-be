At the 1961 National Film Awards, Mughal-e-Azam won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi.[96] In the 1961 Filmfare Awards, Mughal-e-Azam was nominated in seven categories: Best Film, Best Director (Asif), Best Actress (Madhubala),[19] Best Playback Singer (Mangeshkar), Best Music (Naushad),[97] Best Cinematography (Mathur), and Best Dialogue (Aman, Wajahat Mirza, Kamaal Amrohi, and Ehsan Rizvi), winning the awards for Best Film, Best Cinematography, and Best Dialogue.[98][99]
