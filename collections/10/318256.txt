Rivalries, particularly between U.S. interests and the British, complicated a system that already had complex favoritism.[29] As economic activity increased and industries thrived, industrial workers began organizing for better conditions. With the expansion of Mexican agriculture, landless peasants were forced to work for low wages or move to the cities. Peasant agriculture was under pressure as haciendas expanded, such as in the state of Morelos, just south of Mexico City, where sugar plantations burgeoned. There was what one scholar has called “agrarian compression,” but the regions of greatest repression were not the ones that rebelled.[30]
