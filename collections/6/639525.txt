While in Europe Huse represented the Confederate War Dept. and Ordnance Bureau throughout the entire war and arranged for credit to be extended when funds were short.[45] These men also acted as liaisons with Charles Prioleau of Fraser, Trenholm & Co. in Liverpool. Through him they would procure the vessels and arrange for the shipment of goods to the Confederacy. Bulloch would work in close correspondence with Confederate Secretary of the Navy Stephen Mallory in the procurement of several British-made blockade-running vessels.[40][46]
