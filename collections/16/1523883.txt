On January 10, 1941, Germany and the Soviet Union signed an agreement that settled several ongoing issues.[140] The agreement formally set the border between Germany and the Soviet Union between the Igorka river and the Baltic Sea,[141] It extended trade regulation of the 1940 German-Soviet Commercial Agreement until August 1, 1942, increased deliveries above the levels of year one of that agreement,[141] settled trading rights in the Baltics and Bessarabia, calculated the compensation for German property interests in the Baltic States now occupied by the Soviets and other issues.[140] It also covered the migration to Germany within two and a half months of ethnic Germans and German citizens in Soviet-held Baltic territories, and the migration to the Soviet Union of Baltic and "White Russian" "nationals" in German-held territories.[141] Secret protocols in the new agreement stated that Germany would renounce its claims to one piece of Lithuanian territory in the "Secret Additional Protocols" of the Germanâ€“Soviet Boundary and Friendship Treaty and would be paid 7.5 million dollars (31.5 million Reichsmark).[140]
