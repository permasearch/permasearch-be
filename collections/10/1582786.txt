The deadliest fire in California in recorded history was the 1933 Griffith Park Fire in Los Angeles, which killed 29 civilians who tried to fight and/or escape the fire. The second deadliest fire was the 1991 Oakland Hills fire, which killed 25 people. The third deadliest fire was the 2017 Tubbs Fire in Napa and Sonoma Counties, which killed 22 people in and around Santa Rosa.
