In retaliation to what she considered Olivier's "condescending" behavior, Monroe started arriving late and became uncooperative, stating later that "if you don't respect your artists, they can't work well."[182] Her drug use escalated, and according to Spoto she became pregnant and miscarried during the production.[185] She also had arguments with Greene over how MMP should be run, including whether Miller should join the company.[185] Despite the difficulties, the film was completed on schedule by the end of the year.[186] It was released in June 1957 to mixed reviews, and proved unpopular with American audiences.[187] It was better received in Europe, where she was awarded the Italian David di Donatello and the French Crystal Star awards, and was nominated for a BAFTA.[188]
