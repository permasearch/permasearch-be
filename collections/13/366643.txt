The music video was filmed in early January 2015 and it also stars Matt Bennett, who was also Grande's co-star from the Nickelodeon sitcom Victorious. Max Landis also confirmed that one of the voices of the news reporters in the beginning of the video was actress Elizabeth Gillies, who also co-starred in Victorious with Grande and Bennett.[56] Gillies previously appeared Grande's music video for her single "Right There" (2013).[57] Around that time, Max Landis revealed "One Last Time" as Grande's next single after tweeting, "Earth will pass catastrophically through the tail of the comet Eurydice in one week. Gather family and lovers close, one...last...time".[58] The lyric video for "One Last Time" was released on Grande's official Vevo on February 6, 2015, at the same time it was announced that the music video was finished.[59][60] On February 12, 2015, three days before the release of the music video, Grande released a teaser of the music video via Instagram.[61] The music video was visually presented as a found footage, similar to Landis' previous work Chronicle.[62] The "One Last Time" music video was released on February 15, 2015 on Vevo.[63][64] It surpassed 100 million views on June 8, making it Grande's sixth Vevo-certified music video after "Love Me Harder".[65]
