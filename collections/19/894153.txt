The truth of dukkha, "incapable of satisfying,"[web 1] "painful,"[3][5][note 12] is the basic insight that life in this "mundane world,""[web 2] with its clinging and craving to impermanent states and things"[3] is dukkha,[4] unsatisfactory and painful.[web 1][3][5][6][7][web 2] We expect happiness from states and things which are impermanent, and therefore cannot attain real happiness.
