In 2012, Virgin Atlantic carried 5.4 million passengers,[10] making it the seventh-largest UK airline in terms of passenger volume. In the year to 31 December 2013, it reported a £51 million group pre-tax loss (approximately US$87 million),[11] however, in the year to 31 December 2014 the airline reported a return to pre-tax profit of £14.4 million.[12] During July 2017, Virgin Atlantic announced their intention to form a joint venture with Air France-KLM. Under the agreement, Air France-KLM will acquire a 31 percent stake in Virgin Atlantic currently held by Virgin Group for £220 million.[13][14]
