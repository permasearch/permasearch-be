The collusion between local gangs and police officers is a serious problem in many Chinese cities.[35] Local gang bosses make use of personal networks to bribe police officers, and police officers seek corrupt benefits by safegurading their illegal businesses.[36] [37]The most widely publicized case is the Wen Qiang Case.[38] Wen Qiang a deputy police chief, along with family members, was arrested as part of a massive crackdown on corruption of the People's Armed Police and organized crime in Chongqing in late 2009. His crimes included bribes, rape and failing to account for assets; Wen was executed in July 2010.[39] The trials highlighted the continued use of torture by police to obtain confessions,[40] despite laws implemented in June 2010 excluding tortured confessions being used in trials.[41]
