In Italian,[10] Spanish and Portuguese, the conundrum of useless scholarly debates is linked to a similar question of whether angels are sexless or have a sex.[5]  Spanish jurist José Antonio Ramírez López (1908–1987) said about the story of the Byzantine empire: "Everybody knows the idiotic and sometimes bloody discussions in that Empire on the sex of angels, about how many could perch at the same time on the head of a pin".[citation needed]
