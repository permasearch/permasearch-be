In 1936, Gallup successfully predicted that Franklin Roosevelt would defeat Alfred Landon for the U.S. presidency; this event popularized the company and made them a leader in American polling.[4] In 1938, Gallup and Gallup Vice President David Ogilvy began conducting market research for advertising companies and the film industry.[5] In 1958, the modern Gallup Organization was formed when George Gallup grouped all of his polling operations into one organization.[6]
