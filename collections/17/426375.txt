Supporters of the Christian Right have no one unified stance on the role of government since the movement is primarily one that advocates social conservatism; in fact, "struggles [have] broke out in state party organizations" between supporters of the Christian Right and other conservatives.[78][79] It promotes conservative interpretations of the Bible as the basis for moral values, and enforcing such values by legislation. Some members of the Christian right, especially Catholics, accept the Catholic Church's strong support for labor unions.
