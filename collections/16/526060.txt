On May 21, 2012 the leaders of NATO-member countries endorsed an exit strategy during the NATO Summit.[63] ISAF Forces would transfer command of all combat missions to Afghan forces by the middle of 2013,[305] while shifting from combat to advising, training and assisting Afghan security forces.[306][307] Most of the 130,000 ISAF troops would depart by the end of December 2014.[305] A new NATO mission would then assume the support role.[306][308]
