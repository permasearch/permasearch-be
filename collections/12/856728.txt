In the group stage, Chelsea were drawn into Group B, along with Schalke 04 of Germany, Rosenborg of Norway and Spanish club Valencia.[16] Chelsea's first match in the group was against Rosenborg at Stamford Bridge, where they were held to a 1–1 draw.[35] Two days later, manager José Mourinho left Chelsea by mutual consent,[36] and was replaced by their director of football, former Israeli national team coach Avram Grant.[37] Chelsea's second match was against Valencia, whom they beat 2–1, leaving Chelsea with four points from their two matches.[38] Chelsea's next two matches were against Schalke 04. The first match was played at Stamford Bridge, with Chelsea winning 2–0,[39] while the return match in Gelsenkirchen ended in a 0–0 draw.[40] Chelsea's final two matches in their group resulted in a 4–0 victory away to Rosenborg[41] and a 0–0 draw at home to Valencia.[42] Chelsea progressed as group winners with 12 points out of six games.
