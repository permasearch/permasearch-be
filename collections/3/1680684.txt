The Bhagavata is widely recognized as the best-known and most influential of the Puranas and, along with the Itihasa and other puranas, is sometimes referred to as the "Fifth Veda".[27][28][29] It is important in Indian religious literature for its emphasis on the practice of devotion as compared to the more theoretical approach of the Bhagavad Gita; for a definition of religion that competes with the Vedas and for its extended description of a God in human form.[5] It is the source of many popular stories of Krishna's childhood told for centuries on the Indian subcontinent[6] and of legends explaining Hindu festivals such as Holi and Diwali.
