The GS 450h (GWL10), rated at 252 kW (338 hp) and now using an Atkinson cycle design, has a 35 percent improvement in fuel economy over the previous generation GS 450h, and has a higher combined EPA fuel efficiency rating (31 mpg) than the 306 horsepower GS 350 (23 mpg).[37] Trunk space is improved both for the hybrid and standard models.[31] The GS 450h features standard full LED headlamps[38] and a bamboo trimmed interior.[39] The vehicle was unveiled in 2011 Frankfurt Motor Show.[40]
