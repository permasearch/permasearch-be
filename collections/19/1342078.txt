On 20 November 2014, during the visit of Russian Defence Minister Sergey Shoygu led 41 member high-level delegation, Pakistan and Russia signed sign an agreement on military co-operation. Shoygu held in-depth talk with Sharif, afterwards Sharif promised to promote a multi-dimensional relations with Russia.[352] The meeting came months after Russia had lifted an embargo on supplying weapons and military hardware to Pakistan, starting with Mil Mi-24 helicopters.[353] As a sign of improving ties, on 23 September 2016 Russian forces arrived in Pakistan to participate in joint military exercises.[354]
