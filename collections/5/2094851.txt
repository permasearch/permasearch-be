On 10 May 1940, Hitler launched the Battle of France, a major offensive against France and the Low Countries.[124] The SS supplied two of the 89 divisions employed.[125] The LSSAH and elements of the SS-VT participated in the ground invasion of the Battle of the Netherlands.[126] Simultaneously, airborne troops were dropped to capture key Dutch airfields, bridges, and railways. In the five-day campaign, the LSSAH linked up with army units and airborne troops after a number of clashes with Dutch defenders.[126]
