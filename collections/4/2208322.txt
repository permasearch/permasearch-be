The Saudi Civil and Political Rights Association (ACPRA) was created in 2009.[172] One of its co-founders, Mohammed Saleh al-Bejadi, was arbitrarily arrested by Mabahith, the internal security agency, on 21 March 2011, during the 2011 Saudi Arabian protests.[173][174] Al-Bejadi was charged in the Specialized Criminal Court in August 2011 for "insurrection against the ruler, instigating demonstrations, and speaking with foreign [media] channels."[175] Another co-founder, Mohammad Fahad al-Qahtani, was charged for his human rights activities in June 2012.[176][177]
