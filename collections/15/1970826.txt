There are cycles seen within the rainfall data from Northern Europe between Great Britain and Germany, which are seen at a 16-year interval.  Southern Europe experiences a 22-year cycle in rainfall variation.  Other smaller term cycles are seen at 10-12 year and 6-7 year periods within the rainfall record.[39]  Places with significant impact by acid rain across the continent include most of eastern Europe from Poland northward into Scandinavia.[40]
