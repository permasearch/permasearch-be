Trumbo aligned with the Communist Party in the United States before the 1940s, although he did not join the party until 1943.[15][14][16] He was an isolationist. His novel The Remarkable Andrew featured the ghost of President Andrew Jackson appearing to caution the United States against getting involved in World War II. In a review of the book, Time Magazine wise-cracked, "General Jackson's opinions need surprise no one who has observed George Washington and Abraham Lincoln zealously following the Communist Party Line in recent years."[17]
