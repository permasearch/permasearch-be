As with the rest of the South, Georgia is highly religious, with the predominant religion in the state being Christianity. In fact, 85% of Georgians are Christians with 76% of those being Protestant, 8% Catholic and 1% designated as Other; 13% of the population have no religion and 2% are of a religion other than Christianity.[10] Georgia is home to several historic religious sites. Among them are Congregation Mickve Israel,[11] located in Savannah, Georgia; Ebenezer Baptist Church in Atlanta, Georgia;[12] Kiokee Baptist Church located in Appling, Georgia;[13] Shrine of the Black Madonna of Atlanta;[14] and Springfield Baptist Church in Augusta, Georgia.[15]
