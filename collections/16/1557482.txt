Paying college athletes would present several legal issues for the NCAA and its member institutions.[24] If paid, the athletes would lose their amateur status and become university employees.[24] As employees, these athletes would be entitled the National Labor Relations Act to form or join labor organizations and collectively bargain.[24] Advocacy groups for college players could certify as a union given the revenue involved in college athletics."[24] Collegiate sports is not a career or profession, paying college athletes would present issues under Title IX, which requires that institutions accepting federal funds offer equal opportunities to men and women."[24]
