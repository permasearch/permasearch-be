Doug Davidson joined the cast of the show on May 15, 1978.[2][3][4] Its creator, William J. Bell, discovered him during one of Bell's trips to Chicago, while Davidson was modelling for magazines.[5] The actor learned that he had been cast as Paul when a costumer contacted him for measurements.[3] Before joining the soap opera Davidson studied marine biology, abandoning his studies to pursue acting full-time.[6] Former executive producer Wes Kenny had a profound impact on his career: "Wes taught me to listen, respond and to play the situation".[5] In mid-1979 Davidson signed a five-year contract, and began receiving more airtime.[7] In September 1994 he was hired to host The New Price Is Right, which taped down the hall from The Young and the Restless at CBS Television City.[8][9]
