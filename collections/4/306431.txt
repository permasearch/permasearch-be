The 52nd (Lowland) Division was sent to France in early April.[327] The 74th (Yeomanry) Division along with nine British infantry battalions from each of the 10th, 53rd, 60th and 75th Divisions were sent to France, between May and August 1918. What remained of the divisions were reinforced by British Indian Army battalions to reform the divisions.[328][329][330][Note 10] Infantry brigades were reformed with one British battalion and three British Indian Army battalions,[331] except one brigade in the 53rd Division which consisted of one South African and three British Indian Army battalions.[332]
